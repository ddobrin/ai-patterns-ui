The **City of San Marino** ([Italian](/wiki/Italian_language "Italian language"): *Città di San Marino* ), also known simply as **San Marino** and locally as **Città** , is the [capital city](/wiki/Capital_(political) "Capital (political)") of the [Republic of San Marino](/wiki/San_Marino "San Marino") and one of its nine *[castelli](/wiki/Municipalities_of_San_Marino "Municipalities of San Marino")*. It has a population of 4,061.^[\[1\]](#cite_note-pop-1)^ It is on the western slopes of San Marino's highest point, [Monte Titano](/wiki/Monte_Titano "Monte Titano"). It is also the [fifth-least-populated national capital](/wiki/List_of_national_capitals_by_population "List of national capitals by population") in the world.{#cite_ref-pop_1-1}  

Geography {#Geography}
----------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=City_of_San_Marino&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Geography")\]

The City of San Marino borders the *[castelli](/wiki/Municipalities_of_San_Marino "Municipalities of San Marino")* of [Acquaviva](/wiki/Acquaviva_(San_Marino) "Acquaviva (San Marino)"), [Borgo Maggiore](/wiki/Borgo_Maggiore "Borgo Maggiore"), [Fiorentino](/wiki/Fiorentino "Fiorentino"), and [Chiesanuova](/wiki/Chiesanuova "Chiesanuova") and the Italian municipality of [San Leo](/wiki/San_Leo "San Leo").

The City of San Marino contains seven *[curazie](/wiki/Curazia "Curazia")*: [Cà Berlone](/wiki/C%C3%A0_Berlone "Cà Berlone"), [Canepa](/wiki/Canepa,_San_Marino "Canepa, San Marino"), [Casole](/wiki/Casole "Casole"), [Castellaro](/wiki/Castellaro,_San_Marino "Castellaro, San Marino"), [Montalbo](/wiki/Montalbo,_San_Marino "Montalbo, San Marino"), [Murata](/wiki/Murata_(San_Marino) "Murata (San Marino)"), and [Santa Mustiola](/wiki/Santa_Mustiola "Santa Mustiola").^[\[2\]](#cite_note-Elezioni-2)^^[\[3\]](#cite_note-3)^{#cite_ref-3}

[The International Academy of Sciences San Marino](/wiki/International_Academy_of_Sciences_San_Marino "International Academy of Sciences San Marino") was centered here.  

History {#History}
------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=City_of_San_Marino&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: History")\]  
See also: [History of San Marino](/wiki/History_of_San_Marino "History of San Marino")

The city is claimed to be founded by [Saint Marinus](/wiki/Saint_Marinus "Saint Marinus") and several Christian refugees fleeing from [Roman persecution](/wiki/Persecution_of_Christians_in_the_Roman_Empire "Persecution of Christians in the Roman Empire") in the year 301.

The urban heart of the city was protected by [three towers](/wiki/Three_Towers_of_San_Marino "Three Towers of San Marino"): the first, [Guaita](/wiki/Guaita "Guaita"), built in the 11th century, held a reputation for being impenetrable which to a great extent discouraged attacks.

Tensions with bordering powers urged the necessity to build a second tower, [Cesta](/wiki/Cesta "Cesta") (13th century). The defensive system was not completed until the construction of a third tower, the [Montale](/wiki/Montale_(San_Marino) "Montale (San Marino)") (14th century) - the smallest of all and constructed on the last of the summits of Monte Titano.

With the population of the city increasing, the territory of the country was extended by a few square kilometers. Since the Sammarinese policy was not to invade or to use war to obtain new territories, it was by means of purchases and treaties that San Marino obtained the other eight *castelli* which make up the country.  

Economy {#Economy}
------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=City_of_San_Marino&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Economy")\]  
Main article: [Economy of San Marino](/wiki/Economy_of_San_Marino "Economy of San Marino")

The economy of the city of San Marino has always been closely bound to [that of the country](/wiki/Economy_of_San_Marino "Economy of San Marino"). Until recently, the main economic activities of the locality were stone extraction and carving. Today, there is a more varied economy, including tourism, commerce, sale of postage stamps, and a small agricultural industry, although the latter is in decline.

Transport {#Transport}
----------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=City_of_San_Marino&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Transport")\]  
Main article: [Transport in San Marino](/wiki/Transport_in_San_Marino "Transport in San Marino")

The city is known for its long, winding cobblestoned streets, as its altitude and steep approach put it beyond the reach of the [San Marino Highway](/wiki/San_Marino_Highway "San Marino Highway"). San Marino is also notable in that cars are prohibited in much of the city center. A series of [lifts](/wiki/Elevator "Elevator") connects the upper part of city with the lower.

City of San Marino is the top terminus of the [Funivia di San Marino](/wiki/Funivia_di_San_Marino "Funivia di San Marino"), an [aerial cablecar system](/wiki/Aerial_tramway "Aerial tramway") that connects the city to [Borgo Maggiore](/wiki/Borgo_Maggiore "Borgo Maggiore").^[\[4\]](#cite_note-:0-4)^ Running every fifteen minutes, the two-minute ride is renowned for its panoramic views over San Marino, the [Province of Rimini](/wiki/Province_of_Rimini "Province of Rimini"), and the [Adriatic Sea](/wiki/Adriatic_Sea "Adriatic Sea").^[\[4\]](#cite_note-:0-4)^^[\[5\]](#cite_note-:1-5)^ The City of San Marino terminus is located at the turn of Contrada Omagnano with Contrada del Pianello, at the historic city centre's northern end, next to the [Tourism Secretariat](/w/index.php?title=Segreteria_di_Stato_per_il_turismo&action=edit&redlink=1 "Segreteria di Stato per il turismo (page does not exist)") \[[it](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Segreteria_di_Stato_per_il_turismo "it:Segreteria di Stato per il turismo")\] and less than 200 metres (660 feet) from the [Basilica of San Marino](/wiki/Basilica_of_San_Martino "Basilica of San Martino") and the [Piazza della Libertà](/wiki/Piazza_della_Libert%C3%A0,_San_Marino "Piazza della Libertà, San Marino").^[\[6\]](#cite_note-map-6)^{#cite_ref-map_6-0}

The City of San Marino is the terminus of seven of San Marino's eight bus lines, which run to other settlements in the country.^[\[7\]](#cite_note-:03-7)^ Additionally, the central bus stop in Piazzale Marino Calcigni is the terminus of a regular coach connection to [Rimini](/wiki/Rimini "Rimini"),^[\[8\]](#cite_note-:12-8)^^[\[6\]](#cite_note-map-6)^ operated by coach companies Bonelli and Benedettini.^[\[8\]](#cite_note-:12-8)^{#cite_ref-:12_8-1}

Between 1932 and 1944, a 31.5-kilometre (19.6-mile) [electrified](/wiki/Railway_electrification_system "Railway electrification system") [narrow gauge](/wiki/Narrow_gauge "Narrow gauge") railway operated between Rimini and the City of San Marino.^[\[9\]](#cite_note-:123-9)^ During the [Second World War](/wiki/World_War_II "World War II"), the line was bombed and closed,^[\[9\]](#cite_note-:123-9)^^[\[10\]](#cite_note-:142-10)^ after which its tunnels sheltered refugees during the Battles of [Rimini](/wiki/Battle_of_Rimini_(1944) "Battle of Rimini (1944)") and [San Marino](/wiki/Battle_of_San_Marino "Battle of San Marino").^[\[11\]](#cite_note-:132-11)^^[\[12\]](#cite_note-:92-12)^ After the war, the railway was abandoned in favour of the San Marino Highway.^[\[12\]](#cite_note-:92-12)^^[\[13\]](#cite_note-:302-13)^{#cite_ref-:302_13-0}

In 2012, an
800-metre (1⁄2-mile) section was reopened as a [heritage railway](/wiki/Heritage_railway "Heritage railway") in the City of San Marino, running between Piazzale della Stazione and near Via Napoleone.^[\[14\]](#cite_note-:182-14)^ The restored section comprises the original railway's final horseshoe turn through the 502-metre (1,647-foot) Montale tunnel.^[\[15\]](#cite_note-:292-15)^^[\[16\]](#cite_note-:282-16)^{#cite_ref-:282_16-0}
