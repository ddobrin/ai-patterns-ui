**Chișinău** ([/ˌkɪʃɪˈnaʊ/](/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English") [*KISH-in-OW*](/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key"), [US](/wiki/American_English "American English") also [/ˌkiːʃiːˈnaʊ/](/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English") [*KEE-shee-NOW*](/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key"),
Romanian: [\[kiʃiˈnəw\]](/wiki/Help:IPA/Romanian "Help:IPA/Romanian") [](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/transcoded/b/bf/Ro-Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u.ogg/Ro-Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u.ogg.mp3 "Play audio")^[ⓘ](/wiki/File:Ro-Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u.ogg "File:Ro-Chișinău.ogg")^; formerly known as **Kishinev** )^[\[a\]](#cite_note-8)^ is the [capital](/wiki/Capital_city "Capital city") and [largest city](/wiki/List_of_cities_and_towns_in_Moldova "List of cities and towns in Moldova") of [Moldova](/wiki/Moldova "Moldova").^[\[8\]](#cite_note-9)^ The city is Moldova's main industrial and commercial centre, and is located in the middle of the country, on the river [Bîc](/wiki/B%C3%AEc "Bîc"), a tributary of the [Dniester](/wiki/Dniester "Dniester"). According to the results of the [2014 census](/wiki/2014_Moldovan_census "2014 Moldovan census"), the city proper had a population of 532,513, while the population of the Municipality of Chișinău (which includes the city itself and other nearby communities) was 700,000. Chișinău is the most economically prosperous locality in Moldova and its largest transportation hub. Nearly a third of Moldova's population lives in the metro area.{#cite_ref-9}

Moldova has a [history of winemaking](/wiki/Moldovan_wine "Moldovan wine") dating back to at least 3,000 BCE, and as the capital city, Chișinău hosts the yearly national wine festival every October.^[\[9\]](#cite_note-:1-10)^^[\[10\]](#cite_note-:4-11)^ Though the city's buildings were badly damaged during the [Second World War](/wiki/World_War_II "World War II") and earthquakes, there remains a rich architectural heritage, especially in the form of [Socialist realism](/wiki/Socialist_realism "Socialist realism") and [Brutalist architecture](/wiki/Brutalist_architecture "Brutalist architecture"). The city's central railway station boasts a [Russian-Imperial](/wiki/Architecture_of_Russia "Architecture of Russia") architectural style, and maintains direct rail links to Romania. The Swiss-Italian-Russian architect [Alexander Bernardazzi](/wiki/Alexander_Bernardazzi "Alexander Bernardazzi") designed many of the city's buildings, including the [Chișinău City Hall](/wiki/Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u_City_Hall "Chișinău City Hall"), [Church of Saint Theodore](/wiki/St._Teodora_de_la_Sihla_Church "St. Teodora de la Sihla Church"), and the Church of Saint Panteleimon. The city hosts the [National Museum of Fine Arts](/wiki/National_Museum_of_Fine_Arts,_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "National Museum of Fine Arts, Chișinău"), [Moldova State University](/wiki/Moldova_State_University "Moldova State University"), Brancusi Gallery, the [National Museum of History of Moldova](/wiki/National_Museum_of_History_of_Moldova "National Museum of History of Moldova") with over 236,000 exhibits, and bustling markets in the north of the city, including the house where [Alexander Pushkin](/wiki/Alexander_Pushkin "Alexander Pushkin") once resided while in exile from [Alexander I of Russia](/wiki/Alexander_I_of_Russia "Alexander I of Russia"), and which has now been turned into a museum. The city's [Nativity Cathedral](/wiki/Nativity_Cathedral,_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "Nativity Cathedral, Chișinău"), located at the centre of the city and constructed in the 1830s, has been described as a "masterpiece" of [Neoclassical architecture](/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture "Neoclassical architecture").^[\[11\]](#cite_note-12)^{#cite_ref-12}  

Etymology {#Etymology}
----------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Etymology")\]

The origin of the city's name is unclear. A theory suggests that the name may come from the [archaic](/wiki/Archaism "Archaism") Romanian word *chișla* (meaning "spring", "source of water") and *nouă* ("new"), because it was built around a small spring, at the corner of Pușkin and Albișoara streets.^[\[12\]](#cite_note-13)^{#cite_ref-13}

The other version, formulated by (or attributed to^[\[13\]](#cite_note-14)^) [Ștefan Ciobanu](/wiki/%C8%98tefan_Ciobanu "Ștefan Ciobanu"), (occasionally to [Iorgu Iordan](/wiki/Iorgu_Iordan "Iorgu Iordan")) Romanian historian and academician, holds that the name was formed the same way as the name of [Chișineu](/wiki/Chi%C8%99ineu-Cri%C8%99 "Chișineu-Criș") (alternative spelt as *Chișinău* ) in Western [Romania](/wiki/Romania "Romania"), near the border with Hungary. Its Hungarian name is *Kisjenő*, from which the Romanian name originates.^[\[14\]](#cite_note-15)^ Kisjenő comes from *kis* "small" and the Jenő, one of the seven [Hungarian tribes](/wiki/Hungarian_tribes "Hungarian tribes") that entered the [Carpathian Basin](/wiki/Carpathian_Basin "Carpathian Basin") in 896. At least 24 other settlements are named after the Jenő tribe.^[\[15\]](#cite_note-16)^^[\[16\]](#cite_note-17)^{#cite_ref-17}

A third theory by [Kiss Lajos](https://hu.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kiss_Lajos_(nyelv%C3%A9sz) "hu:Kiss Lajos (nyelvész)") linguist and [slavist](/wiki/Slavic_studies "Slavic studies") hold (as possible origin), that the name came from the [cuman](/wiki/Cumans "Cumans") *kešene ("grave", [kurgan](/wiki/Kurgan "Kurgan")) and the [karachayian](/wiki/Karachays "Karachays")* "[cemetery](/wiki/Cemetery "Cemetery")", and these came from the [Persian](/wiki/Persian_language "Persian language") *[kāšāne](https://en.m.wiktionary.org/wiki/%DA%A9%D8%A7%D8%B4%D8%A7%D9%86%D9%87#Persian) ([house](/wiki/House "House")) word.* ^[\[10\]](#cite_note-:4-11)^*[\[1\]](https://m.nyest.hu/hirek/chisinau-is-magyar-volt?fbclid=IwY2xjawEtbRNleHRuA2FlbQIxMQABHSRuOXxjq2BsVPtX6NySq3EiNgVBjRRTZCmKRa-nkTLjva4cR3BK7LXBnQ_aem_RIM2Hyb3h7_lYQbVDnfGVA)*{#cite_ref-:4_11-1}

### Moldavian period {#Moldavian_period}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Moldavian period")\]

Founded in 1436 as a [monastery](/wiki/Monastery "Monastery") village, the city was part of the [Principality of Moldavia](/wiki/Moldavia "Moldavia") (which, starting with the 16th century became a [vassal state of the Ottoman Empire](/wiki/Vassal_and_tributary_states_of_the_Ottoman_Empire "Vassal and tributary states of the Ottoman Empire"), but still retaining its autonomy). At the beginning of the 19th century Chișinău was a small town of 7,000 inhabitants.  

### Russian Imperial period {#Russian_Imperial_period}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Russian Imperial period")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u_1889_1.jpg/220px-Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u_1889_1.jpg)](/wiki/File:Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u_1889_1.jpg) Chișinău, 1889

In 1812, in the aftermath of the [Russo-Turkish War (1806--1812)](/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_(1806%E2%80%931812) "Russo-Turkish War (1806–1812)"), the eastern half of Moldavia [was ceded](/wiki/Treaty_of_Bucharest_(1812) "Treaty of Bucharest (1812)") by the Ottomans to the [Russian Empire](/wiki/Russian_Empire "Russian Empire"). The newly acquired territories became known as [Bessarabia](/wiki/Bessarabia "Bessarabia").

Under Russian government, Chișinău became the capital of the newly annexed [oblast](/wiki/Oblast "Oblast") (later [guberniya](/wiki/Governorate_(Russia) "Governorate (Russia)")) of [Bessarabia](/wiki/Bessarabia_Governorate "Bessarabia Governorate"). By 1834, an [imperial](/wiki/Russian_Revival_architecture "Russian Revival architecture") townscape with broad and long roads had emerged as a result of a generous [development plan](/wiki/Development_plan "Development plan"), which divided Chișinău roughly into two areas: the old part of the town, with its irregular building structures, and a newer city centre and station. Between 26 May 1830 and 13 October 1836 the architect [Avraam Melnikov](/wiki/Avraam_Melnikov "Avraam Melnikov") established the [Catedrala Nașterea Domnului](/wiki/Nativity_Cathedral,_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "Nativity Cathedral, Chișinău") with a magnificent bell tower. In 1840 the building of the [Triumphal Arch](/wiki/Triumphal_Arch,_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "Triumphal Arch, Chișinău"), planned by the architect Luca Zaushkevich, was completed. Following this the construction of numerous buildings and landmarks began.

On 28 August 1871, Chișinău was linked by [rail](/wiki/Calea_Ferat%C4%83_din_Moldova "Calea Ferată din Moldova") with [Tiraspol](/wiki/Tiraspol "Tiraspol"), and in 1873 with [Cornești](/wiki/Corne%C8%99ti_(town),_Ungheni "Cornești (town), Ungheni"). Chișinău-[Ungheni](/wiki/Ungheni "Ungheni")-[Iași](/wiki/Ia%C8%99i "Iași") railway was opened on 1 June 1875 in preparation for the [Russo-Turkish War (1877--1878)](/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_(1877%E2%80%931878) "Russo-Turkish War (1877–1878)"). The town played an important part in the war between Russia and the [Ottoman Empire](/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire"), as the main [staging area](/wiki/Staging_area "Staging area") of the Russian invasion. During the [Belle Époque](/wiki/Belle_%C3%89poque "Belle Époque"), the mayor of the city was [Carol Schmidt](/wiki/Carol_Schmidt "Carol Schmidt"), whose contribution to the modernisation of the city is still commemorated by Moldovans.^[\[18\]](#cite_note-19)^^[\[19\]](#cite_note-publika-20)^ Its population had grown to 92,000 by 1862, and to 125,787 by 1900.^[\[20\]](#cite_note-21)^{#cite_ref-21}  

#### Pogroms and pre-revolution {#Pogroms_and_pre-revolution}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Pogroms and pre-revolution")\]  
Main article: [Kishinev pogrom](/wiki/Kishinev_pogrom "Kishinev pogrom")

In the late 19th century, especially due to growing anti-Semitic sentiment in the [Russian Empire](/wiki/Russian_Empire "Russian Empire") and better economic conditions in Moldova, many Jews chose to settle in Chișinău. By the year 1897, 46% of the population of Chișinău was Jewish, over 50,000 people.^[\[21\]](#cite_note-22)^{#cite_ref-22}

As part of the pogrom wave organized in the Russian Empire, a large [anti-Semitic](/wiki/Antisemitism "Antisemitism") riot was organized in the town on 19--20 April 1903, which would later be known as the [Kishinev pogrom](/wiki/Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u_pogrom_(1903) "Chișinău pogrom (1903)"). The rioting continued for three days, resulting in 47 Jews dead, 92 severely wounded, and 500 suffering minor injuries. In addition, several hundred houses and many businesses were plundered and destroyed.^[\[22\]](#cite_note-23)^ Some sources say 49 people were killed.^[\[23\]](#cite_note-24)^ The pogroms are largely believed to have been incited by anti-Jewish propaganda in the only official newspaper of the time, *Bessarabetz* (*Бессарабецъ* ). Mayor Schmidt disapproved of the incident and resigned later in 1903. The reactions to this incident included a petition to [Tsar](/wiki/Tsar "Tsar") [Nicholas II of Russia](/wiki/Nicholas_II_of_Russia "Nicholas II of Russia") on behalf of [the American people](/wiki/United_States "United States") by US President [Theodore Roosevelt](/wiki/Theodore_Roosevelt "Theodore Roosevelt") in July 1903.^[\[24\]](#cite_note-pogrom-25)^{#cite_ref-pogrom_25-0}

On 22 August 1905, another violent event occurred: the police opened fire on an estimated 3,000 demonstrating agricultural workers. Only a few months later, on 19--20 October 1905, a further protest occurred, helping to force the hand of Nicholas II in bringing about the [October Manifesto](/wiki/October_Manifesto "October Manifesto"). However, these demonstrations suddenly turned into [another anti-Jewish pogrom](/wiki/Second_Kishinev_pogrom "Second Kishinev pogrom"), resulting in 19 deaths.^[\[24\]](#cite_note-pogrom-25)^{#cite_ref-pogrom_25-1}  

### Romanian period {#Romanian_period}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Romanian period")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Monumentul_a_lui_Stefan_cel_Mare%2C_Chisinau_Stefan_the_Great_Monument%2C_Chisinau%2C_Moldova_%2849601054843%29.jpg/220px-Monumentul_a_lui_Stefan_cel_Mare%2C_Chisinau_Stefan_the_Great_Monument%2C_Chisinau%2C_Moldova_%2849601054843%29.jpg)](/wiki/File:Monumentul_a_lui_Stefan_cel_Mare,_Chisinau_Stefan_the_Great_Monument,_Chisinau,_Moldova_(49601054843).jpg) [Stephen the Great](/wiki/Stephen_the_Great "Stephen the Great") monument

Following the Russian [October Revolution](/wiki/October_Revolution "October Revolution"), Bessarabia declared independence from the crumbling empire, as the [Moldavian Democratic Republic](/wiki/Moldavian_Democratic_Republic "Moldavian Democratic Republic"), before joining the [Kingdom of Romania](/wiki/Kingdom_of_Romania "Kingdom of Romania"). As of 1919, Chișinău, with an estimated population of 133,000,^[\[25\]](#cite_note-Kaba-26)^ became the second largest city in Romania.{#cite_ref-Kaba_26-0}

Between 1918 and 1940, the center of the city undertook large [renovation](/wiki/Renovation "Renovation") work. Romania granted important subsidies to its province and initiated large scale investment programs in the infrastructure of the main cities in Bessarabia, expanded the railroad infrastructure and started an extensive program to eradicate illiteracy.

In 1927, the [Stephen the Great Monument](/wiki/Stephen_the_Great_Monument "Stephen the Great Monument"), by the sculptor [Alexandru Plămădeală](/wiki/Alexandru_Pl%C4%83m%C4%83deal%C4%83 "Alexandru Plămădeală"), was erected. In 1933, the first higher education institution in Bessarabia was established, by transferring the Agricultural Sciences Section of the [University of Iași](/wiki/University_of_Ia%C8%99i "University of Iași") to Chișinău, as the [Faculty of Agricultural Sciences](/wiki/Agricultural_State_University_of_Moldova "Agricultural State University of Moldova").  

### World War II {#World_War_II}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: World War II")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Moldova-chisineu-war-memorial-site.jpg/220px-Moldova-chisineu-war-memorial-site.jpg)](/wiki/File:Moldova-chisineu-war-memorial-site.jpg) *Eternity* -- a memorial complex dedicated to the soldiers who fell in World War II and the [military conflict in Transnistria](/wiki/War_in_Transnistria "War in Transnistria") [![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Trenul_durerii.jpg/220px-Trenul_durerii.jpg)](/wiki/File:Trenul_durerii.jpg) [Train of Pain](/wiki/Memorial_to_Victims_of_Stalinist_Repression "Memorial to Victims of Stalinist Repression") -- the monument to the victims of communist mass deportations in Moldova [![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/State_Art_Museum_%2880-ies%29._%288257398062%29.jpg/220px-State_Art_Museum_%2880-ies%29._%288257398062%29.jpg)](/wiki/File:State_Art_Museum_(80-ies)._(8257398062).jpg) State Art Museum, during the [Cold War](/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War") period [![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Prospectul_P%C4%83cii_%281980%29.jpg/220px-Prospectul_P%C4%83cii_%281980%29.jpg)](/wiki/File:Prospectul_P%C4%83cii_(1980).jpg) Prospectul Păcii in 1980

On 28 June 1940, as a direct result of the [Molotov--Ribbentrop Pact](/wiki/Molotov%E2%80%93Ribbentrop_Pact "Molotov–Ribbentrop Pact"), Bessarabia was [annexed by the Soviet Union](/wiki/Soviet_occupation_of_Bessarabia_and_Northern_Bukovina "Soviet occupation of Bessarabia and Northern Bukovina") from Romania, and Chișinău became the capital of the newly created [Moldavian Soviet Socialist Republic](/wiki/Moldavian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Moldavian Soviet Socialist Republic").

Following the Soviet occupation, mass deportations, linked with atrocities, were executed by the [NKVD](/wiki/NKVD "NKVD") between June 1940 and June 1941. More than 400 people were [summarily executed](/wiki/Summarily_executed "Summarily executed") in Chișinău in July 1940 and buried in the grounds of the Metropolitan Palace, the Chișinău Theological Institute, and the backyard of the Italian Consulate, where the NKVD had established its headquarters.^[\[26\]](#cite_note-Brez2010-27)^ As part of the policy of political repression of the potential opposition to the Communist power, tens of thousand members of native families were [deported from Bessarabia](/wiki/Soviet_deportations_from_Bessarabia_and_Northern_Bukovina "Soviet deportations from Bessarabia and Northern Bukovina") to other regions of the USSR.{#cite_ref-Brez2010_27-0}

[A devastating earthquake](/wiki/1940_Vrancea_earthquake "1940 Vrancea earthquake") occurred on 10 November 1940, measuring 7.4 (or 7.7, according to other sources) on the [Richter scale](/wiki/Richter_scale "Richter scale"). The [epicenter](/wiki/Epicenter "Epicenter") of the quake was in the [Vrancea Mountains](/wiki/Vrancea_Mountains "Vrancea Mountains"), and it led to substantial destruction: 78 deaths and 2,795 damaged buildings (of which 172 were destroyed).^[\[27\]](#cite_note-28)^^[\[28\]](#cite_note-29)^{#cite_ref-29}

In June 1941, in order to recover Bessarabia, Romania entered [World War II](/wiki/World_War_II "World War II") under the command of the German [Wehrmacht](/wiki/Wehrmacht "Wehrmacht"), declaring [war on the Soviet Union](/wiki/Romania_in_World_War_II "Romania in World War II"). Chișinău was severely affected in the chaos of the Second World War. In June and July 1941, the city came under bombardment by Nazi [air raids](/wiki/Strategic_bombing "Strategic bombing"). However, the Romanian and newly Moldovan sources assign most of the responsibility for the damage to Soviet NKVD [destruction battalions](/wiki/Destruction_battalion "Destruction battalion"), which operated in Chișinău until 17 July 1941, when it was captured by Axis forces.^[\[29\]](#cite_note-30)^{#cite_ref-30}

During the German and Romanian military administration, the city suffered from the [Nazi extermination policy](/wiki/Holocaust "Holocaust") of its Jewish inhabitants, who were transported on trucks to the outskirts of the city and then summarily shot in partially dug pits. The number of Jews murdered during the initial occupation of the city is estimated at 10,000 people.^[\[30\]](#cite_note-31)^ During this time, Chișinău, part of [Lăpușna County](/wiki/L%C4%83pu%C8%99na_County_(Romania) "Lăpușna County (Romania)"), was the capital of the newly established [Bessarabia Governorate](/wiki/Bessarabia_Governorate_(Romania) "Bessarabia Governorate (Romania)") of Romania.^[\[31\]](#cite_note-32)^{#cite_ref-32}

As the war drew to a conclusion, the city was once again the scene of heavy fighting as German and Romanian troops retreated. Chișinău was captured by the Red Army on 24 August 1944 as a result of the [Second Jassy--Kishinev offensive](/wiki/Second_Jassy%E2%80%93Kishinev_offensive "Second Jassy–Kishinev offensive").  

### Soviet period {#Soviet_period}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Soviet period")\]

After the war, [Bessarabia](/wiki/Bessarabia "Bessarabia") was fully reintegrated into the Soviet Union, with around 65 percent of its territory as the Moldavian SSR, while the remaining 35 percent was transferred to the [Ukrainian SSR](/wiki/Ukrainian_SSR "Ukrainian SSR").

Two other waves of deportations of Moldova's native population were carried out by the Soviets, the first one immediately after the Soviet reoccupation of Bessarabia until the end of the 1940s and the second one in the mid-1950s.^[\[32\]](#cite_note-33)^^[\[33\]](#cite_note-34)^{#cite_ref-34}
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/LOWA_ET54.jpg/220px-LOWA_ET54.jpg)](/wiki/File:LOWA_ET54.jpg) Trams in Chișinău (pictured Gothawagen ET54) were discontinued in 1961.

In the years 1947 to 1949, the architect [Alexey Shchusev](/wiki/Alexey_Shchusev "Alexey Shchusev") developed a plan with the aid of a team of architects for the gradual reconstruction of the city. ^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^

There was rapid population growth in the 1950s, to which the Soviet administration responded by constructing large-scale housing and palaces in the style of [Stalinist architecture](/wiki/Stalinist_architecture "Stalinist architecture"). This process continued under [Nikita Khrushchev](/wiki/Nikita_Khrushchev "Nikita Khrushchev"), who called for construction under the slogan "good, cheaper, and built faster." The new [architectural style](/wiki/Architectural_style "Architectural style") brought about dramatic change and generated the style that dominates today, with large [blocks of flats](/wiki/Blocks_of_flats "Blocks of flats") arranged in considerable settlements. ^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^ These Khrushchev-era buildings are often informally called [Khrushchyovka](/wiki/Khrushchyovka "Khrushchyovka").

The period of the most significant [redevelopment of the city](/wiki/Urban_renewal "Urban renewal") began in 1971, when the [Council of Ministers](/wiki/Council_of_Ministers_(Soviet_Union) "Council of Ministers (Soviet Union)") of the Soviet Union adopted a decision "On the measures for further development of the city of Kishinev," which secured more than one billion [rubles](/wiki/Soviet_ruble "Soviet ruble") in investment from the [state budget](/wiki/Budget "Budget"),^[\[34\]](#cite_note-35)^ and continued until the [independence of Moldova](/wiki/Independence_of_Moldova "Independence of Moldova") in 1991. The share of dwellings built during the Soviet period (1951--1990) represents 74.3 percent of total households.^[\[35\]](#cite_note-36)^{#cite_ref-36}

On 4 March 1977, the city was again jolted by a devastating [earthquake](/wiki/1977_Vrancea_earthquake "1977 Vrancea earthquake"). Several people were killed, and panic broke out.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^ The [Intourist Hotel](/wiki/National_Hotel_(Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u) "National Hotel (Chișinău)"), a flagship property constructed by the [Soviet state-owned travel monopoly of the same name](/wiki/Intourist "Intourist"), was completed in 1978.^[\[36\]](#cite_note-37)^{#cite_ref-37}

On 22 April 1993, the city inaugurated the Monument to the Victims of Jewish Ghettos, a public monument centring on a bronze statue of the [Biblical prophet](/wiki/Prophet "Prophet") [Moses](/wiki/Moses "Moses"), which serves as a symbol of remembrance to the thousands of Jews who perished during the holocaust. The monument was designed by architect Simeon Shoihet and sculptor Naum Epelbaum. It stands on Ierusalim Street, marking the site of the main entrance to the Chișinău ghetto, which was established in the lower part of the city in July 1941, shortly after the German and Romanian troops occupied the area.^[\[37\]](#cite_note-38)^^\[*[unreliable source?](/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources "Wikipedia:Reliable sources")*\]^{#cite_ref-38}  

### After independence {#After_independence}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: After independence")\]

Since [Moldovan independence](/wiki/Independence_Day_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova "Independence Day of the Republic of Moldova") following the [dissolution of the Soviet Union](/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union "Dissolution of the Soviet Union"), many streets of Chișinău have been [renamed after historic persons, places or events](/wiki/Street_names_in_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "Street names in Chișinău"). Independence from the Soviet Union was followed by a large-scale renaming of streets and localities from a Communist theme into a national one.^[\[38\]](#cite_note-39)^{#cite_ref-39}

On 5 September 2022, the country's first Christian university Universitatea Moldo-Americană opened its doors, supported by the Scandinavian broadcaster [Visjon Norge](/wiki/Visjon_Norge "Visjon Norge") and several donors in [Norway](/wiki/Norway "Norway"), and run in cooperation with the [American Southeastern University](/wiki/Southeastern_University_(Florida) "Southeastern University (Florida)") in [Florida](/wiki/Florida "Florida"), [United States](/wiki/United_States "United States").^[\[39\]](#cite_note-:2-40)^{#cite_ref-:2_40-0}

Following [Russia's invasion of Ukraine](/wiki/Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine "Russian invasion of Ukraine"), Moldova allowed more than 600,000 Ukrainian civilians to flee Ukraine across their border. Despite being among the [poorest states in Europe](/wiki/Poverty_in_Moldova "Poverty in Moldova"), Moldova has continued to host more than 100,000 [Ukrainian refugees](/wiki/Ukrainian_refugee_crisis_(2022%E2%80%93present) "Ukrainian refugee crisis (2022–present)"), many of them in Chișinău.^[\[40\]](#cite_note-41)^^[\[41\]](#cite_note-42)^^[\[42\]](#cite_note-43)^{#cite_ref-43}

On 23 November 2022, the Chișinău Court of Appeal ruled that [Chișinău International Airport](/wiki/Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u_International_Airport "Chișinău International Airport") will return to state ownership, according to [justice minister](/wiki/Justice_Minister_of_Moldova "Justice Minister of Moldova") [Sergiu Litvinenco](/wiki/Sergiu_Litvinenco "Sergiu Litvinenco"), more than three months after an international court allowed Moldova to terminate a 49-year concession deal with airport operator Avia Invest.^[\[43\]](#cite_note-44)^ In April 2023, the [Dutch government](/wiki/Dutch_Government "Dutch Government") opened a new embassy in Chișinău.^[\[44\]](#cite_note-45)^{#cite_ref-45}

On 21 May 2023, tens of thousands of Moldovans took to the streets in a massive rally, the [European Moldova National Assembly](/wiki/European_Moldova_National_Assembly "European Moldova National Assembly"), to support the country's [European Union membership bid](/wiki/Accession_of_Moldova_to_the_European_Union "Accession of Moldova to the European Union").^[\[45\]](#cite_note-46)^^[\[46\]](#cite_note-47)^ Moldovan police said more than 75,000 demonstrators were present at the rally organised by Moldovan president [Maia Sandu](/wiki/Maia_Sandu "Maia Sandu").^[\[47\]](#cite_note-48)^{#cite_ref-48}

Later that month, Chișinău hosted a major international summit of the [European Political Community](/wiki/European_Political_Community "European Political Community") organised to discuss the illegal Russian invasion of Ukraine as well as cybersecurity, migration and energy security, and regional issues in [Azerbaijan](/wiki/Azerbaijan "Azerbaijan"), [Armenia](/wiki/Armenia "Armenia"), and [clashes in Kosovo](/wiki/2022%E2%80%932023_North_Kosovo_crisis "2022–2023 North Kosovo crisis").^[\[48\]](#cite_note-49)^^[\[49\]](#cite_note-50)^^[\[50\]](#cite_note-:0-51)^{#cite_ref-:0_51-0}  

Geography {#Geography}
----------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: Geography")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Biserica_de_lemn_din_Hiri%C8%99eni_3.jpg/220px-Biserica_de_lemn_din_Hiri%C8%99eni_3.jpg)](/wiki/File:Biserica_de_lemn_din_Hiri%C8%99eni_3.jpg) "Museum of the village" (*Muzeul satului*), located on the southern outskirts of the city

Chișinău is located on the river [Bâc](/wiki/B%C3%A2c_River "Bâc River"), a tributary of the [Dniester](/wiki/Dniester "Dniester"), at [47°0′N 28°55′E﻿ / ﻿47.000°N 28.917°E﻿ / 47.000; 28.917](https://geohack.toolforge.org/geohack.php?pagename=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&params=47_0_N_28_55_E_), with an area of 120 km^2^ (46 sq mi). The municipality comprises 635 km^2^ (245 sq mi).

The city lies in central Moldova and is surrounded by a relatively level landscape with very fertile ground.

Chișinău is roughly equidistant between the borders with Romania (58 km.) and Ukraine (54 km.), and between the northernmost (188 km.) and southernmost (179 km.) points of Moldova, thus meaning that it is very close to Moldova's geographic centre.  

### Climate {#Climate}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Climate")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Gradina_botanica_Chisinau_%2815%29.jpg/220px-Gradina_botanica_Chisinau_%2815%29.jpg)](/wiki/File:Gradina_botanica_Chisinau_(15).jpg) [Botanical garden](/wiki/Chisinau_Botanical_Garden "Chisinau Botanical Garden")

Chișinău has a [humid continental climate](/wiki/Humid_continental_climate "Humid continental climate") ([Köppen climate classification](/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification "Köppen climate classification") *Dfa* ) characterised by warm summers and cold, windy winters. Winter minimum temperatures are often below 0 °C (32 °F), although they rarely drop below −10 °C (14 °F). In summer, the average maximum temperature is approximately 25 °C (77 °F), however, temperatures occasionally reach 35 to 40 °C (95 to 104 °F) in mid-summer in downtown. Although average [humidity](/wiki/Humidity "Humidity") during summer is relatively low, most of the annual [precipitation](/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology) "Precipitation (meteorology)") occurs during summer, causing infrequent yet heavy storms.

Spring and autumn temperatures vary between 16 and 24 °C (61 and 75 °F), and precipitation during this time tends to be lower than in summer but with more frequent yet milder periods of rain.

With a population of 662,836 inhabitants (as of 2014), the Municipality of Chișinău (which includes the nearby communities) is the largest of these municipalities.^[\[53\]](#cite_note-54)^{#cite_ref-54}

Besides the city itself, the municipality comprises 34 other suburban localities: 6 towns (containing further 2 villages within), and 12 communes (containing further 14 villages within). Economy {#Economy}
------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=18 "Edit section: Economy")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Malldova_Shopping%2C_%D0%9A%D0%B8%D1%88%D0%B8%D0%BD%D0%B5%D0%B2%2C_%D0%A0%D0%B5%D1%81%D0%BF%D1%83%D0%B1%D0%BB%D0%B8%D0%BA%D0%B0_%D0%9C%D0%BE%D0%BB%D0%B4%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B0_Malldova_Shopping%2C_Chisinau%2C_Republica_Moldova_Moldova_Shopping%2C_Chisinau%2C_Republic_of_Moldova_%2851187661202%29.jpg/220px-thumbnail.jpg)](/wiki/File:Malldova_Shopping,_%D0%9A%D0%B8%D1%88%D0%B8%D0%BD%D0%B5%D0%B2,_%D0%A0%D0%B5%D1%81%D0%BF%D1%83%D0%B1%D0%BB%D0%B8%D0%BA%D0%B0_%D0%9C%D0%BE%D0%BB%D0%B4%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B0_Malldova_Shopping,_Chisinau,_Republica_Moldova_Moldova_Shopping,_Chisinau,_Republic_of_Moldova_(51187661202).jpg) [MallDova](/wiki/Malldova "Malldova") shopping centre

Historically, the city was home to fourteen factories in 1919.^[\[25\]](#cite_note-Kaba-26)^ Chișinău is the financial and business capital of Moldova. Its GDP comprises about 60% of the national economy^[\[55\]](#cite_note-56)^ reached in 2012 the amount of 52 billion lei (US$4 billion). Thus, the GDP per capita of Chișinău stood at 227% of the Moldova's average. Chișinău has the largest and most developed mass media sector in Moldova, and is home to several related companies ranging from leading television networks and radio stations to major newspapers. All national and international banks (15) have their headquarters located in Chișinău.{#cite_ref-56}

Notable sites around Chișinău include Cineplex Loteanu, the new malls [MallDova](/wiki/Malldova "Malldova"), Port Mall and best-known retailers, such as N1, Linella, Kaufland, Fourchette and Metro. While many locals continue to shop at the [bazaars](/wiki/Bazaar "Bazaar"), many upper class residents and tourists shop at the retail stores and at MallDova. Jumbo, an older mall in the Botanica district, and Sun City, in the centre, are more popular with locals.

Several amusement parks exist around the city. A [Soviet](/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union")-era one is located in the Botanica district, along the three lakes of a major park, which reaches the outskirts of the city centre. Another, the modern Aventura Park, is located farther from the centre. The Chișinău State Circus, which used to be in a grand building in the Râșcani sector, has been inactive for several years due to a poorly funded renovation project.^[\[56\]](#cite_note-57)^{#cite_ref-57}

Chișinău's growth plan was developed in the 19th century. In 1836 the construction of the [Kishinev Cathedral](/wiki/Kishinev_Cathedral "Kishinev Cathedral") and its [belfry](/wiki/Bell_tower "Bell tower") was finished. The belfry was demolished in Soviet times and was rebuilt in 1997. Chișinău also displays a tremendous number of Orthodox churches and 19th-century buildings around the city such as [Ciuflea Monastery](/wiki/Ciuflea_Monastery "Ciuflea Monastery") or the [Transfiguration Church](/wiki/Transfiguration_Church,_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "Transfiguration Church, Chișinău"). Much of the city is made from limestone quarried from [Cricova](/wiki/Cricova "Cricova"), leaving a famous [wine cellar](/wiki/Cricova_(winery) "Cricova (winery)") there.

Many modern-style buildings have been built in the city since 1991. There are many office and shopping complexes that are modern, renovated or newly built, including Kentford, SkyTower, and [Unión Fenosa](/wiki/Uni%C3%B3n_Fenosa "Unión Fenosa") headquarters. However, the old Soviet-style clusters of living blocks are still an extensive feature of the cityscape.

### Education {#Education}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=26 "Edit section: Education")\]

The city is home to 9 public and 8 private universities, the [Academy of Sciences of Moldova](/wiki/Academy_of_Sciences_of_Moldova "Academy of Sciences of Moldova"), a number of institutions offering high school and 1--2 years of [college education](/wiki/Higher_education "Higher education"). Among them are [Moldova State University](/wiki/Moldova_State_University "Moldova State University"), the [Academy of Economic Studies of Moldova](/wiki/Academy_of_Economic_Studies_of_Moldova "Academy of Economic Studies of Moldova"), [Alexandru cel Bun Military Academy](/wiki/Alexandru_cel_Bun_Military_Academy "Alexandru cel Bun Military Academy"), [Nicolae Testemițanu State University of Medicine and Pharmacy](/wiki/Nicolae_Testemi%C8%9Banu_State_University_of_Medicine_and_Pharmacy "Nicolae Testemițanu State University of Medicine and Pharmacy"), and [Ion Creangă State Pedagogical University](/wiki/Ion_Creang%C4%83_State_Pedagogical_University "Ion Creangă State Pedagogical University").

On 5 September 2022, the country's first Christian university Universitatea Moldo-Americană opened its doors, supported by the Scandinavian broadcaster [Visjon Norge](/wiki/Visjon_Norge "Visjon Norge") and several donors in [Norway](/wiki/Norway "Norway"), and run in cooperation with the [American Southeastern University](/wiki/Southeastern_University_(Florida) "Southeastern University (Florida)") in [Florida](/wiki/Florida "Florida"), [United States](/wiki/United_States "United States").^[\[39\]](#cite_note-:2-40)^{#cite_ref-:2_40-1}

In Chișinău there are several museums. The three national museums are the National Museum of Ethnography and Natural History, the [National Museum of Fine Arts](/wiki/National_Museum_of_Fine_Arts,_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "National Museum of Fine Arts, Chișinău"), and the [National Museum of History of Moldova](/wiki/National_Museum_of_History_of_Moldova "National Museum of History of Moldova"). The National Museum of Ethnography and Natural History was founded in October 1889 by baron Alexandru Stuart, moved to its current location in 1905, and is the oldest museum in Moldova.^[\[60\]](#cite_note-61)^ It houses more than 135,000 exhibit pieces, among them a life-sized reconstruction of the skeleton of a dinothere, discovered in the Rezine region in 1966.^[\[61\]](#cite_note-62)^ It also includes exhibits on [natural history](/wiki/Natural_history "Natural history"), [natural sciences](/wiki/Natural_science "Natural science"), [archaeology](/wiki/Archaeology "Archaeology"), [paleontology](/wiki/Paleontology "Paleontology"), [geology](/wiki/Geology "Geology"), and [ethnography](/wiki/Ethnography "Ethnography").^[\[62\]](#cite_note-63)^ The building was designed by the architect [Vladimir Tsyganko](/wiki/Vladimir_Tsyganko "Vladimir Tsyganko") in a distinctive [Moorish architectural style](/wiki/Moorish_architecture "Moorish architecture") with a signature frontal façade consisting of a triangular pediment supported by two [Doric columns](/wiki/Doric_order "Doric order").^[\[63\]](#cite_note-64)^^[\[64\]](#cite_note-65)^{#cite_ref-65}

The [National Library of Moldova](/wiki/National_Library_of_Moldova "National Library of Moldova") is also located in Chișinău.^[\[65\]](#cite_note-66)^{#cite_ref-66}

### Events and festivals {#Events_and_festivals}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=27 "Edit section: Events and festivals")\]

Chișinău, as well as Moldova as a whole, still show signs of ethnic culture. Signs that say "Patria Mea" (English: My homeland) can be found all over the capital. While few people still wear traditional Moldavian attire, large public events often draw in such original costumes.

*Moldova National Wine Day* and *Wine Festival* take place every year in the first weekend of October, in Chișinău. The events celebrate the autumn harvest and recognises the country's long history of winemaking, which dates back to at least 3,000 BCE.^[\[66\]](#cite_note-67)^^[\[67\]](#cite_note-68)^ Moldova has been called the wine capital of Europe and its yearly festival is a major cultural and tourist event, and every year the streets are filled with people enjoying food, wine, dance, and music taking over the streets.^[\[9\]](#cite_note-:1-10)^^[\[68\]](#cite_note-:3-69)^ Moldova's most-awarded sommelier Mihai Druta has described Moldovan wine as being about "small producers and family wineries making premium wine. And nothing costs more than 100 Euro a bottle."^[\[68\]](#cite_note-:3-69)^ *[The Daily Express](/wiki/Daily_Express "Daily Express")* in 2019 described the city as "Europe's latest hotspot" in which journalist Maisha Frost praised "its wines, monumental wineries and their epic tasting sessions."^[\[69\]](#cite_note-70)^ She described the city's Carpe Diem [wine bar](/wiki/Wine_bar "Wine bar") as "the flagship for a flourishing new breed of craft-style makers."{#cite_ref-70}  

Media {#Media}
--------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=28 "Edit section: Media")\]

The majority of Moldova's [media industry](/wiki/Mass_media "Mass media") is based in Chișinău. There are almost 30 FM-radio stations and 10 TV-channels broadcasting in Chișinău. The first radio station in Chișinău, [Radio Basarabia](/wiki/Radio_Basarabia "Radio Basarabia"), was launched by the [Romanian Radio Broadcasting Company](/wiki/Romanian_Radio_Broadcasting_Company "Romanian Radio Broadcasting Company") on 8 October 1939, when the religious service was broadcast on air from the [Nativity Cathedral](/wiki/Nativity_Cathedral,_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "Nativity Cathedral, Chișinău"). The first TV station in the city, [Moldova 1](/wiki/Moldova_1 "Moldova 1"), was launched on 30 April 1958, while [Nicolae Lupan](/wiki/Nicolae_Lupan "Nicolae Lupan") was serving as the redactor-in-chief of [TeleRadio-Moldova](/wiki/TeleRadio-Moldova "TeleRadio-Moldova").^[\[70\]](#cite_note-71)^{#cite_ref-71}

The state national broadcaster in the country is the [state-owned](/wiki/Public_ownership "Public ownership") [Moldova 1](/wiki/Moldova_1 "Moldova 1"), which has its head office in the city. The broadcasts of [TeleradioMoldova](/wiki/TeleRadio-Moldova "TeleRadio-Moldova") have been criticised by the Independent Journalism Center as showing 'bias' towards the authorities.^[\[71\]](#cite_note-72)^{#cite_ref-72}

Other TV channels based in Chișinău are [Pro TV Chișinău](/wiki/Pro_TV_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "Pro TV Chișinău"), [PRIME](/wiki/Prime_(Moldovan_TV_channel) "Prime (Moldovan TV channel)"), [Jurnal TV](/wiki/Jurnal_TV "Jurnal TV"), [Publika TV](/wiki/Publika_TV "Publika TV"), CTC, [DTV](/wiki/DTV_(Moldovan_TV_channel) "DTV (Moldovan TV channel)"), Euro TV, [TV8](/wiki/TV8_(Moldova) "TV8 (Moldova)"), etc. In addition to television, most Moldovan radio and newspaper companies have their headquarters in the city. Broadcasters include the national radio [Vocea Basarabiei](/wiki/Vocea_Basarabiei "Vocea Basarabiei"), Prime FM, BBC Moldova, Radio Europa Libera, Kiss FM Chișinău, Pro FM Chișinău, Radio 21, Fresh FM, Radio Nova, [Russkoye Radio](/wiki/Russkoye_Radio "Russkoye Radio"), Hit FM Moldova, and many others.

The biggest broadcasters are SunTV, [StarNet](/wiki/StarNet "StarNet") (IPTV), [Moldtelecom](/wiki/Moldtelecom "Moldtelecom") (IPTV), Satellit and Zebra TV. In 2007 SunTV and Zebra launched [digital TV](/wiki/Digital_television "Digital television") cable networks.  
Further information on the defunct newspaper founded in 1933: [Mișcarea femenistă](/wiki/Mi%C8%99carea_femenist%C4%83 "Mișcarea femenistă")

### Road {#Road}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=33 "Edit section: Road")\]

The most popular form of internal [transport in Moldova](/wiki/Transport_in_Moldova "Transport in Moldova") is generally the bus.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^ Although the city has just three main terminals, buses generally serve as the [means of transport](/wiki/Mode_of_transport "Mode of transport") between cities in and outside of Moldova. Popular destinations include [Tiraspol](/wiki/Tiraspol "Tiraspol"), [Odesa](/wiki/Odesa "Odesa") (Ukraine), [Iași](/wiki/Ia%C8%99i "Iași") and [Bucharest](/wiki/Bucharest "Bucharest") (Romania).  

### Rail {#Rail}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=34 "Edit section: Rail")\]

The second most popular form of domestic transportation within Moldova is via railways. The total length of the network managed by [Moldovan Railway](/wiki/Calea_Ferat%C4%83_din_Moldova "Calea Ferată din Moldova") (as of 2009^[\[update\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit)^) is 1,232 kilometres (766 miles). The entire network is single track and is not electrified. The central hub of all railways is [Chișinău Central Railway Station](/wiki/Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u_Railway_Station "Chișinău Railway Station"). There is another smaller railway station -- [Revaca](/wiki/Revaca_railway_station "Revaca railway station") located on the city's ends.

Chișinău Railway Station has an international railway terminal with connections to [Bucharest](/wiki/Bucharest "Bucharest"), [Kyiv](/wiki/Kyiv "Kyiv"), [Minsk](/wiki/Minsk "Minsk"), [Odesa](/wiki/Odesa "Odesa"), Moscow, [Samara](/wiki/Samara,_Russia "Samara, Russia"), [Varna](/wiki/Varna,_Bulgaria "Varna, Bulgaria") and [St. Petersburg](/wiki/St._Petersburg "St. Petersburg"). Due to the simmering conflict between Moldova and the unrecognised [Transnistria republic](/wiki/Transnistria "Transnistria") the rail traffic towards Ukraine is occasionally stopped.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^  

### Public transport {#Public_transport}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=35 "Edit section: Public transport")\]  

#### Trolleybuses {#Trolleybuses}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=36 "Edit section: Trolleybuses")\]  
See also: [Trolleybuses in Chișinău](/wiki/Trolleybuses_in_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "Trolleybuses in Chișinău")

There is wide [trolleybus](/wiki/Trolleybus "Trolleybus") network operating as common public transportation within city. From 1994, Chișinău saw the establishment of new trolleybus lines, as well as an increase in capacity of existing lines, to improve connections between the urban districts. The network comprises 22 trolleybus lines being 246 km (153 mi) in length. Trolleybuses run between 05:00 and 03:00. There are 320 units daily operating in Chișinău. However the requirements are as minimum as 600 units.^\[*[clarification needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify "Wikipedia:Please clarify")*\]^ A trolleybus ticket costs 6 lei (ca. $0.31). It is the cheapest method of transport within Chișinău municipality.  

#### Buses {#Buses}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=37 "Edit section: Buses")\]

There are 29 lines of buses within Chișinău municipality. At each public transportation stops there is attached a schedule for buses and trolleybuses. There are approximately 330 public transportation stops within Chișinău municipality. There is a big lack of buses inside city limits, with only 115 buses operating within Chișinău.^[\[73\]](#cite_note-74)^{#cite_ref-74}  

#### Minibuses {#Minibuses}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=38 "Edit section: Minibuses")\]

In Chișinău and its suburbs, privately operated minibuses known as "*[rutieras](/wiki/Rutiera "Rutiera")* " generally follow the major bus and [trolleybus](/wiki/Trolleybus "Trolleybus") routes and appear more frequently.^[\[74\]](#cite_note-75)^{#cite_ref-75}

As of October 2017, there are 1,100 units of minibuses operating within Chișinău. Minibuses services are priced the same as buses -- 3 [lei](/wiki/Moldovan_leu "Moldovan leu") for a ticket (ca. $0.18).^[\[75\]](#cite_note-76)^{#cite_ref-76}  

### Traffic {#Traffic}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=39 "Edit section: Traffic")\]

The city traffic becomes more congested as each year passes. Nowadays there are about 300,000 cars in the city plus 100,000 transit transports coming to the city each day.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^ The number of personal transports is expected to reach 550,000 (without transit) by 2025.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^  

Sport {#Sport}
--------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&action=edit&section=40 "Edit section: Sport")\]

[Football](/wiki/Association_football "Association football") is the most widely followed sport in Chișinău. Local clubs such as [Zimbru](/wiki/FC_Zimbru_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "FC Zimbru Chișinău") and [Dacia Buiucani](/wiki/Dacia_Buiucani "Dacia Buiucani") compete in the [Moldovan Super Liga](/wiki/Moldovan_National_Division "Moldovan National Division") (first division), while [Real Succes](/wiki/Real_Succes_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "Real Succes Chișinău") and [Victoria Bardar](/wiki/Victoria_Bardar "Victoria Bardar") play in the [Liga 1](/wiki/Moldovan_%22A%22_Division "Moldovan \"A\" Division") (second division).

[Zimbru Stadium](/wiki/Zimbru_Stadium "Zimbru Stadium"), which opened in May 2006 with a seating capacity of 10,500, meets all the requirements for hosting official international matches and serves as the home venue for the [Moldova national football team](/wiki/Moldova_national_football_team "Moldova national football team").

Since 2011, [CS Femina-Sport Chișinău](/wiki/CS_Femina-Sport_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "CS Femina-Sport Chișinău") has organized women's competitions in seven sports.

[Arena Chișinău](/wiki/Arena_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "Arena Chișinău"), an [indoor arena](/wiki/Indoor_arena "Indoor arena") was opened in 2022.

The [FMF Beach Soccer Arena](/wiki/FMF_Beach_Soccer_Arena "FMF Beach Soccer Arena"), a [beach soccer](/wiki/Beach_soccer "Beach soccer") stadium that opened in 2022, was the main venue for the [Socca EuroCup](/wiki/Socca_EuroCup "Socca EuroCup") events held in 2023 and 2024.^[\[76\]](#cite_note-socca1-77)^{#cite_ref-socca1_77-0}

The city also hosts the annual [Chișinău International Marathon](/wiki/Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u_International_Marathon "Chișinău International Marathon").
