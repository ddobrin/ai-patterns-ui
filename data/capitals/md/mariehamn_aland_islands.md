**Mariehamn** ([/məˈriːəhɑːmən/](/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English") [*mə-REE-ə-hah-mən*](/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key"),^[\[9\]](#cite_note-9)^
Finland Swedish: [\[mɑriːeˈhɑmn\]](/wiki/Help:IPA/Swedish "Help:IPA/Swedish") [](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/transcoded/9/9e/Mariehamn.ogg/Mariehamn.ogg.mp3 "Play audio")^[ⓘ](/wiki/File:Mariehamn.ogg "File:Mariehamn.ogg")^; [Finnish](/wiki/Finnish_language "Finnish language"): *Maarianhamina* [\[ˈmɑːriɑnˌhɑminɑ\]](/wiki/Help:IPA/Finnish "Help:IPA/Finnish"); [Latin](/wiki/Latin_language "Latin language"): *Portus Mariae* ) is the [capital](/wiki/Capital_city "Capital city") of [Åland](/wiki/%C3%85land "Åland"), an autonomous territory under [Finnish](/wiki/Finland "Finland") [sovereignty](/wiki/Sovereignty "Sovereignty"). Mariehamn is the seat of the [Government](/wiki/Government_of_%C3%85land "Government of Åland") and [Parliament of Åland](/wiki/Parliament_of_%C3%85land "Parliament of Åland"), and 40% of the population of Åland live in the city. It is mostly surrounded by [Jomala](/wiki/Jomala "Jomala"), the second-largest municipality in Åland in terms of population; to the east, it is bordered by [Lemland](/wiki/Lemland "Lemland"). Like the rest of Åland, Mariehamn is unilingually [Swedish-speaking](/wiki/Swedish-speaking_population_of_Finland "Swedish-speaking population of Finland") and around 82% of the inhabitants speak it as their native language.^[\[5\]](#cite_note-population_by_language-5)^{#cite_ref-population_by_language_5-1}

The theme of the [coat of arms](/wiki/Coat_of_arms "Coat of arms") of Mariehamn refers to the city's main livelihood, [maritime transport](/wiki/Maritime_transport "Maritime transport"), and the city's parks, which are typically lined with [linden trees](/wiki/Tilia "Tilia").^[\[10\]](#cite_note-10)^^[\[11\]](#cite_note-11)^^[\[12\]](#cite_note-12)^^[\[13\]](#cite_note-13)^ The coat of arms was designed by Nils Byman and confirmed in 1951.^[\[14\]](#cite_note-14)^{#cite_ref-14}

Due to its central location in the [Baltic Sea](/wiki/Baltic_Sea "Baltic Sea"), Mariehamn has become a major summer [resort town](/wiki/Resort_town "Resort town") for global [tourism](/wiki/Tourism "Tourism"); as many as 1.5 million tourists visit annually.^[\[15\]](#cite_note-15)^^[\[16\]](#cite_note-16)^^[\[17\]](#cite_note-17)^{#cite_ref-17}  

History {#History}
------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mariehamn&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")\]

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Mariehamn_map.svg/220px-Mariehamn_map.svg.png)](/wiki/File:Mariehamn_map.svg) Map of Mariehamn with the pre-1961 territory of the town marked with hatched outline

The town was named after the Russian empress [Maria Alexandrovna](/wiki/Maria_Alexandrovna_(Marie_of_Hesse) "Maria Alexandrovna (Marie of Hesse)") (1824--1880), literally meaning "Marie's Port". Mariehamn was founded on 21 February 1861 around the village of [Övernäs](/w/index.php?title=%C3%96vern%C3%A4s&action=edit&redlink=1 "Övernäs (page does not exist)"),^[\[2\]](#cite_note-sjöfartspromenaden-2)^^[\[18\]](#cite_note-18)^ in what was at the time part of the municipality of [Jomala](/wiki/Jomala "Jomala"). The city has since expanded and incorporated more of Jomala territory. Mariehamn was built according to a very regular scheme which is well-preserved. One of the oldest streets is *Södragatan* where many wooden houses dating from the 19th century can be seen.{#cite_ref-18}

In the mid 1800's, the business of cargo shipping started to catch on, and many important shipowners and shipyards took their residence in the developing city.^[\[19\]](#cite_note-19)^{#cite_ref-19}

Following the [First World War](/wiki/First_World_War "First World War"), Mariehamn was home to the Mariehamn Grain Fleet.^[\[20\]](#cite_note-SWW-20)^{#cite_ref-SWW_20-0}

On November 8, 1963, a [devastating plane crash](/wiki/Aero_Flight_217 "Aero Flight 217") occurred in Mariehamn, in which 22 out of 25 people lost their lives, which made it the second-deadliest aviation accident in the history of Finland.^[\[21\]](#cite_note-21)^{#cite_ref-21}

### Harbours {#Harbours}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mariehamn&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Harbours")\]

The city is located on a [peninsula](/wiki/Peninsula "Peninsula"). It has two important harbours, one located on the western shore and one on the eastern shore, which are ice-free for nearly the whole year, and have no tides.^[\[22\]](#cite_note-protheropage5-22)^ The [Western Harbour](/wiki/Western_Harbour_(Mariehamn) "Western Harbour (Mariehamn)") is an important international harbour with daily traffic to [Sweden](/wiki/Sweden "Sweden"), [Estonia](/wiki/Estonia "Estonia") and mainland [Finland](/wiki/Finland "Finland"). A powerful incentive for Baltic ferries to stop at Mariehamn is that, with respect to indirect taxation, Åland is not part of the [EU customs zone](/wiki/EU_customs_zone "EU customs zone") and so duty-free goods can be sold aboard. Åland and Mariehamn have a reputable heritage in shipping. The [Flying P-Liner](/wiki/Flying_P-Liner "Flying P-Liner") *[Pommern](/wiki/Pommern_(ship) "Pommern (ship)")* [museum ship](/wiki/Museum_ship "Museum ship") (part of the [Åland Maritime Museum](/wiki/%C3%85land_Maritime_Museum "Åland Maritime Museum")) is anchored in the Western Harbour. The Eastern Harbour features one of the largest marinas in [Scandinavia](/wiki/Scandinavia "Scandinavia"). The famous Dutch steamer *Jan Nieveen* (now called *[F.P. von Knorring](/wiki/F.P._von_Knorring_(ship) "F.P. von Knorring (ship)")* , after Åland teacher and vicar [Frans Peter von Knorring](/wiki/Frans_Peter_von_Knorring "Frans Peter von Knorring")) can also be found here.{#cite_ref-protheropage5_22-0}  

### Airport {#Airport}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mariehamn&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Airport")\]

[Mariehamn Airport](/wiki/Mariehamn_Airport "Mariehamn Airport") serves the city; it has scheduled flights on two airlines including [Finnair](/wiki/Finnair "Finnair").  

### Roads {#Roads}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mariehamn&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Roads")\]

At three of Åland's four highways, Mariehamn serves as a starting point for [Main Road 1](/wiki/%C3%85land_Highway_1 "Åland Highway 1") to [Eckerö](/wiki/Ecker%C3%B6 "Eckerö"), [Main Road 2](/wiki/%C3%85land_Highway_2 "Åland Highway 2") to [Sund](/wiki/Sund,_%C3%85land "Sund, Åland") and [Main Road 3](/wiki/%C3%85land_Highway_3 "Åland Highway 3") to [Lumparland](/wiki/Lumparland "Lumparland").  

Culture {#Culture}
------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mariehamn&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Culture")\]

The city is an important centre for Åland media; both of the local newspapers ([Ålandstidningen](/wiki/%C3%85landstidningen "Ålandstidningen") and [Nya Åland](/wiki/Nya_%C3%85land "Nya Åland")), several radio stations and the local TV channels ([TV Åland](/wiki/TV_%C3%85land "TV Åland") and [Åland24](/wiki/%C3%85land24 "Åland24")) operate out of the city. The islanders are traditionally fond of reading, and had public libraries before 1920. A printing works was established in the town in 1891.^[\[22\]](#cite_note-protheropage5-22)^ The municipal library, which was built in 1989, is one of the most interesting modern buildings.{#cite_ref-protheropage5_22-1}

### Architects {#Architects}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mariehamn&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Architects")\]

Mariehamn features several buildings drawn by Finnish architect [Lars Sonck](/wiki/Lars_Sonck "Lars Sonck"), who moved to Åland as a child. Buildings drawn by him include the church of Mariehamn (1927), the main building of the [Åland Maritime College](/w/index.php?title=%C3%85land_Maritime_College&action=edit&redlink=1 "Åland Maritime College (page does not exist)") (1927) and the town hall (1939). [Hilda Hongell](/wiki/Hilda_Hongell "Hilda Hongell") also designed several buildings, although only a few are still standing.  

### The Church of St George {#The_Church_of_St_George}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mariehamn&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: The Church of St George")\]

The main parish church of Mariehamn was opened in 1927 and has been expanded in 1959 and renovated in 1972. when the pews were painted blue. The visible decorations include painted wooden ceiling panels, one for each of the followers of Christ. The unnamed panel is that representing Judas Iscariot. The church paintings and decorations are the work of Finnish artist Bruno Tuukkanen. These include the golden mosaics behind the altar which show the life of Christ. Gifts to the church include the font and the ship that hangs from the ceiling. This is the *Tjerimaj* . The church houses a 37 stop organ built in stages (1969, 1982).^[\[23\]](#cite_note-23)^{#cite_ref-23}

### Climate {#Climate}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mariehamn&action=edit&section=13 "Edit section: Climate")\]

Mariehamn has a transitional climate between a [humid continental climate](/wiki/Humid_continental_climate "Humid continental climate") ([Dfb](/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification "Köppen climate classification")) and an [oceanic climate](/wiki/Oceanic_climate "Oceanic climate") ([Cfb](/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification "Köppen climate classification")) as a result of the strong maritime moderation from being an island in the Baltic Sea. This causes summers to be cooler than both the Swedish and Finnish mainlands, with winters being similar in cold to the adjacent coastal part of Sweden but milder than Finland's mainland. The lowest temperature at Mariehamn airport was −32.9 °C (−27.2 °F) in February 1979, and the highest temperature on record was 31.3 °C (88.3 °F) in July 2022 and July 1941.^[\[27\]](#cite_note-Suomen_Maakuntien_ilmasto-Ilmatieteenlaitos-27)^{#cite_ref-Suomen_Maakuntien_ilmasto-Ilmatieteenlaitos_27-0}
