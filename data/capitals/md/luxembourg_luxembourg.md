**Luxembourg** ([Luxembourgish](/wiki/Luxembourgish_language "Luxembourgish language"): *Lëtzebuerg* ; [French](/wiki/French_language "French language"): *Luxembourg* ; [German](/wiki/German_language "German language"): *Luxemburg* ),^[\[pron 1\]](#cite_note-1)^ also known as **Luxembourg City** (Luxembourgish: *Stad Lëtzebuerg* or *d'Stad*; French: *Ville de Luxembourg* ; German: *Stadt Luxemburg* or *Luxemburg-Stadt*),^[\[pron 2\]](#cite_note-2)^ is the [capital city](/wiki/Capital_city "Capital city") of [Luxembourg](/wiki/Luxembourg "Luxembourg") and the [country's most populous commune](/wiki/Communes_of_Luxembourg "Communes of Luxembourg"). Standing at the confluence of the [Alzette](/wiki/Alzette "Alzette") and [Pétrusse](/wiki/P%C3%A9trusse "Pétrusse") rivers in southern Luxembourg, the city lies at the heart of [Western Europe](/wiki/Western_Europe "Western Europe"), situated 213 km (132 mi) by road from [Brussels](/wiki/Brussels "Brussels") and 209 km (130 mi) from [Cologne](/wiki/Cologne "Cologne").^[\[1\]](#cite_note-3)^ The city contains [Luxembourg Castle](/wiki/Luxembourg_Castle "Luxembourg Castle"), established by the [Franks](/wiki/Franks "Franks") in the [Early Middle Ages](/wiki/Early_Middle_Ages "Early Middle Ages"), around which a settlement developed.{#cite_ref-3}

As of 31 December 2024^[\[update\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit)^, Luxembourg City has a population of 136,208 inhabitants,^[\[2\]](#cite_note-4)^ which is more than three times the population of the country's second most populous commune ([Esch-sur-Alzette](/wiki/Esch-sur-Alzette "Esch-sur-Alzette")). The population consists of 160 nationalities. Foreigners represent 70.4% of the city's population, whilst Luxembourgers represent 29.6% of the population; the number of foreign-born residents in the city rises steadily each year.^[\[3\]](#cite_note-5)^{#cite_ref-5}

In 2024, Luxembourg was ranked by the [IMF](/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund "International Monetary Fund") as having the highest GDP per capita in the world at $140,310 (PPP),^[\[4\]](#cite_note-6)^ with the city having developed into a banking and administrative centre. In the 2019 Mercer worldwide survey of 231 cities, Luxembourg was placed first for personal safety, while it was ranked 18th for quality of living.^[\[5\]](#cite_note-7)^{#cite_ref-7}

Luxembourg is one of the *de facto* [capitals of the European Union](/wiki/Capital_of_the_European_Union "Capital of the European Union") (alongside [Brussels](/wiki/Brussels "Brussels"), [Frankfurt](/wiki/Frankfurt "Frankfurt") and [Strasbourg](/wiki/Strasbourg "Strasbourg")), as it is the seat of several [institutions](/wiki/Institutions_of_the_European_Union "Institutions of the European Union"), [agencies](/wiki/Agencies_of_the_European_Union "Agencies of the European Union") and [bodies](/wiki/Bodies_of_the_European_Union "Bodies of the European Union"), including the [Court of Justice of the European Union](/wiki/Court_of_Justice_of_the_European_Union "Court of Justice of the European Union"), the [European Court of Auditors](/wiki/European_Court_of_Auditors "European Court of Auditors"), the [Secretariat of the European Parliament](/wiki/Secretariat_of_the_European_Parliament "Secretariat of the European Parliament"), the [European Public Prosecutor's Office](/wiki/European_Public_Prosecutor%27s_Office "European Public Prosecutor's Office"), the [European Investment Bank](/wiki/European_Investment_Bank "European Investment Bank"), the [European Investment Fund](/wiki/European_Investment_Fund "European Investment Fund"), the [European Stability Mechanism](/wiki/European_Stability_Mechanism "European Stability Mechanism"), [Eurostat](/wiki/Eurostat "Eurostat"), as well as other [European Commission](/wiki/European_Commission "European Commission") departments and services.^[\[6\]](#cite_note-EU-8)^ The [Council of the European Union](/wiki/Council_of_the_European_Union "Council of the European Union") meets in the city for three months annually.^[\[6\]](#cite_note-EU-8)^{#cite_ref-EU_8-1}  

History {#History}
------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")\]  
For a chronological guide, see [Timeline of Luxembourg City](/wiki/Timeline_of_Luxembourg_City "Timeline of Luxembourg City").  
See also: [Fortress of Luxembourg](/wiki/Fortress_of_Luxembourg "Fortress of Luxembourg")
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/LASB_K_Hellwig_1053.jpg/220px-LASB_K_Hellwig_1053.jpg)](/wiki/File:LASB_K_Hellwig_1053.jpg) Map of the City around 1600 [![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Luxembourg_City_Night_Wikimedia_Commons.jpg/220px-Luxembourg_City_Night_Wikimedia_Commons.jpg)](/wiki/File:Luxembourg_City_Night_Wikimedia_Commons.jpg) The Old City of Luxembourg at night

In the [Roman](/wiki/Roman_Empire "Roman Empire") era, a fortified tower guarded the crossing of two [Roman roads](/wiki/Roman_road "Roman road") that met at the site of Luxembourg city. Through an exchange treaty with the abbey of [Saint Maximin](/wiki/Maximin_of_Trier "Maximin of Trier") in [Trier](/wiki/Trier "Trier") in 963, [Siegfried I](/wiki/Siegfried_I_of_Luxembourg "Siegfried I of Luxembourg") of the [Ardennes](/wiki/Ardennes "Ardennes"), a close relative of [King Louis II](/wiki/Louis_the_Stammerer "Louis the Stammerer") of [France](/wiki/Kingdom_of_France "Kingdom of France") and [Emperor Otto the Great](/wiki/Otto_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor "Otto I, Holy Roman Emperor"), acquired the feudal lands of Luxembourg. Siegfried built his castle, named *Lucilinburhuc* ("small castle"), on the [Bock](/wiki/Bock_(Luxembourg) "Bock (Luxembourg)") *Fiels* ("rock"), mentioned for the first time in the aforementioned exchange treaty.

In 987, Archbishop [Egbert](/wiki/Egbert,_Archbishop_of_Trier "Egbert, Archbishop of Trier") of [Trier](/wiki/Archbishopric_of_Trier "Archbishopric of Trier") consecrated five altars in the Church of the Redemption (today St. Michael's Church). At a Roman road intersection near the church, a marketplace appeared around which the city developed.

The city, because of its location and natural geography, has through history been a place of strategic military significance. The first fortifications were built as early as the 10th century. By the end of the 12th century, as the city expanded westward around the new St. Nicholas Church (today the [Cathedral of Notre Dame](/wiki/Notre-Dame_Cathedral,_Luxembourg "Notre-Dame Cathedral, Luxembourg")), new walls were built that included an area of 5 hectares (12 acres). In about 1340, under the reign of [John the Blind](/wiki/John_of_Luxembourg "John of Luxembourg"), new fortifications were built that stood until 1867.

In 1443, the [Burgundians](/wiki/Duchy_of_Burgundy "Duchy of Burgundy") under [Philip the Good](/wiki/Philip_the_Good "Philip the Good") conquered Luxembourg. Luxembourg became part of the Burgundian, and later [Spanish](/wiki/Spanish_Empire "Spanish Empire") and [Austrian](/wiki/Habsburg_monarchy "Habsburg monarchy") empires (See [Spanish Netherlands](/wiki/Spanish_Netherlands "Spanish Netherlands") and [Spanish Road](/wiki/Spanish_Road "Spanish Road")) and under those Habsburg administrations Luxembourg Castle was repeatedly strengthened so that by the 16th century, Luxembourg itself was one of the strongest fortifications in Europe. Subsequently, the Burgundians, the Spanish, the French, the Spanish again, the Austrians, the French again, and the [Prussians](/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia "Kingdom of Prussia") conquered Luxembourg.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^

In the 17th century, the first [casemates](/wiki/Casemate "Casemate") were built; initially, Spain built 23 km (14 mi) of tunnels, starting in 1644.^[\[7\]](#cite_note-9)^ These were then enlarged under French rule by [Marshal Vauban](/wiki/Vauban "Vauban"), and augmented again under Austrian rule in the 1730s and 1740s.{#cite_ref-9}

During the [French Revolutionary Wars](/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars "French Revolutionary Wars"), the city was occupied by [France](/wiki/French_First_Republic "French First Republic") twice: once, briefly, in 1792--93, and, later, after a seven-month [siege](/wiki/Siege_of_Luxembourg_(1794%E2%80%9395) "Siege of Luxembourg (1794–95)").^[\[8\]](#cite_note-Kreins_2003,_p._64-10)^ Luxembourg held out for so long under the French siege that French politician and military engineer [Lazare Carnot](/wiki/Lazare_Carnot "Lazare Carnot") called Luxembourg "the best [fortress](/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") in the world, except Gibraltar", giving rise to the city's nickname: the '[Gibraltar of the North](/wiki/Gibraltar_of_the_North "Gibraltar of the North")'.^[\[8\]](#cite_note-Kreins_2003,_p._64-10)^{#cite_ref-Kreins_2003,_p._64_10-1}

Nonetheless, the Austrian garrison eventually surrendered, and as a consequence, Luxembourg was annexed by the French Republic, becoming part of the [département](/wiki/D%C3%A9partement "Département") of [Forêts](/wiki/For%C3%AAts "Forêts"), with Luxembourg City as its [préfecture](/wiki/Pr%C3%A9fecture "Préfecture"). Under the 1815 [Treaty of Paris](/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1815) "Treaty of Paris (1815)"), which ended the [Napoleonic Wars](/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars "Napoleonic Wars"), Luxembourg City was placed under [Prussian](/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia "Kingdom of Prussia") military control as a part of the [German Confederation](/wiki/German_Confederation "German Confederation"), although sovereignty passed to the [House of Orange-Nassau](/wiki/House_of_Orange-Nassau "House of Orange-Nassau"), in [personal union](/wiki/Personal_union "Personal union") with the [United Kingdom of the Netherlands](/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands "United Kingdom of the Netherlands").

After the [Luxembourg Crisis](/wiki/Luxembourg_Crisis "Luxembourg Crisis"), the 1867 [Treaty of London](/wiki/Treaty_of_London_(1867) "Treaty of London (1867)") required Luxembourg to dismantle the fortifications in Luxembourg City. Their demolition took sixteen years, cost 1.5 million gold [francs](/wiki/French_franc "French franc"), and required the destruction of over 24 km (15 mi) of underground defences and 4 hectares (9.9 acres) of [casemates](/wiki/Casemate "Casemate"), [batteries](/wiki/Artillery_battery "Artillery battery"), [barracks](/wiki/Barracks "Barracks"), etc.^[\[9\]](#cite_note-11)^ Furthermore, the Prussian [garrison](/wiki/Garrison "Garrison") was to be withdrawn.^[\[10\]](#cite_note-12)^{#cite_ref-12}
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/HS20_Al_Br%C3%A9ck.JPG/220px-HS20_Al_Br%C3%A9ck.JPG)](/wiki/File:HS20_Al_Br%C3%A9ck.JPG) The [Passerelle](/wiki/Passerelle_(Luxembourg) "Passerelle (Luxembourg)"), also known as the viaduct or old bridge, overlooking the [Pétrusse](/wiki/P%C3%A9trusse "Pétrusse") river valley. It opened in 1861.

When, in 1890, [Grand Duke William III](/wiki/William_III_of_the_Netherlands "William III of the Netherlands") died without any male heirs, the Grand Duchy passed out of Dutch hands, and into an independent line under [Grand Duke Adolphe](/wiki/Adolphe,_Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg "Adolphe, Grand Duke of Luxembourg"). Thus, Luxembourg, which had hitherto been independent in theory only, became a truly independent country, and Luxembourg City regained some of the importance that it had lost in 1867 by becoming the capital of a fully independent state.

Despite Luxembourg's best efforts to remain [neutral](/wiki/Neutral_country "Neutral country") in the First World War, it was [occupied by Germany](/wiki/German_occupation_of_Luxembourg_during_World_War_I "German occupation of Luxembourg during World War I") on 2 August 1914. On 30 August, [Helmuth von Moltke](/wiki/Helmuth_von_Moltke_the_Younger "Helmuth von Moltke the Younger") moved his headquarters to Luxembourg City, closer to his armies in France in preparation for a swift victory. However, the victory never came, and Luxembourg would play host to the German high command for another four years. At the end of the occupation, Luxembourg City was the scene of an attempted [communist](/wiki/Communist "Communist") revolution; on 9 November 1918, communists declared a [socialist republic](/wiki/Socialist_republic "Socialist republic"), but it lasted only a few hours.^[\[11\]](#cite_note-13)^{#cite_ref-13}

In 1921, the [city limits](/wiki/City_limits "City limits") were greatly expanded. The communes of [Eich](/wiki/Eich,_Luxembourg "Eich, Luxembourg"), [Hamm](/wiki/Hamm,_Luxembourg "Hamm, Luxembourg"), [Hollerich](/wiki/Hollerich "Hollerich"), and [Rollingergrund](/wiki/Rollingergrund "Rollingergrund") were incorporated into Luxembourg City, making the city the largest commune in the country (a position that it would hold until 1978).

In 1940, [Germany occupied Luxembourg again](/wiki/German_occupation_of_Luxembourg_during_World_War_II "German occupation of Luxembourg during World War II"). The [Nazis](/wiki/Nazi_Germany "Nazi Germany") were not prepared to allow Luxembourgers self-government, and gradually integrated Luxembourg into the Third Reich by informally attaching the country administratively to a neighbouring German province. Under the occupation, the capital city's streets all received new, German names, which was announced on 4 October 1940.^[\[12\]](#cite_note-:0-14)^ The Avenue de la Liberté for example, a major road leading to the railway station, was renamed "Adolf-Hitlerstraße".^[\[12\]](#cite_note-:0-14)^ Luxembourg City was liberated on 10 September 1944.^[\[13\]](#cite_note-15)^ The city was under long-range bombardment by the German [V-3 cannon](/wiki/V-3_cannon "V-3 cannon") in December 1944 and January 1945.{#cite_ref-15}

After the war, Luxembourg ended its neutrality, and became a founding member of several inter-governmental and supra-governmental institutions. In 1952, the city became the headquarters of the High Authority of the [European Coal and Steel Community](/wiki/European_Coal_and_Steel_Community "European Coal and Steel Community"). In 1967, the High Authority was merged with the commissions of the other European institutions; although Luxembourg City was no longer the seat of the ECSC, it hosted some part-sessions of the [European Parliament](/wiki/European_Parliament "European Parliament") until 1981.^[\[14\]](#cite_note-16)^ Luxembourg remains the seat of the European Parliament's secretariat, as well as the [Court of Justice of the European Union](/wiki/Court_of_Justice_of_the_European_Union "Court of Justice of the European Union"), the [European Court of Auditors](/wiki/European_Court_of_Auditors "European Court of Auditors"), and the [European Investment Bank](/wiki/European_Investment_Bank "European Investment Bank"). Several departments of the [European Commission](/wiki/European_Commission "European Commission") are also based in Luxembourg.^[\[6\]](#cite_note-EU-8)^ The [Council of the EU](/wiki/Council_of_the_EU "Council of the EU") meets in the city for the months of April, June and October annually.^[\[6\]](#cite_note-EU-8)^{#cite_ref-EU_8-3}

### Topography {#Topography}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Topography")\]

Luxembourg City lies on the southern part of the [Luxembourg plateau](/wiki/Luxembourg_plateau "Luxembourg plateau"), a large [Early Jurassic](/wiki/Early_Jurassic "Early Jurassic") [sandstone](/wiki/Sandstone "Sandstone") formation that forms the heart of the [Gutland](/wiki/Gutland_(Luxembourg) "Gutland (Luxembourg)"), a low-lying and flat area that covers the southern two-thirds of the country.

The city centre occupies a picturesque site on a salient, perched high on precipitous cliffs that drop into the narrow valleys of the [Alzette](/wiki/Alzette "Alzette") and [Pétrusse](/wiki/P%C3%A9trusse "Pétrusse") rivers, whose confluence is in Luxembourg City. The 70 m (230 ft) deep gorges cut by the rivers are spanned by many bridges and [viaducts](/wiki/Viaduct "Viaduct"), including the [Adolphe Bridge](/wiki/Adolphe_Bridge "Adolphe Bridge"), the [Grand Duchess Charlotte Bridge](/wiki/Grand_Duchess_Charlotte_Bridge "Grand Duchess Charlotte Bridge"), and the [Passerelle](/wiki/Passerelle_(Luxembourg) "Passerelle (Luxembourg)"). Although Luxembourg City is not particularly large, its layout is complex, as the city is set on several levels, straddling hills and dropping into the two gorges.

The commune of Luxembourg City covers an area of over 51 km^2^ (20 sq mi), or 2% of the Grand Duchy's total area. This makes the city the [fourth-largest commune](/wiki/List_of_communes_of_Luxembourg_by_area "List of communes of Luxembourg by area") in Luxembourg, and by far the largest [urban area](/wiki/Urban_area "Urban area"). Luxembourg City is not particularly densely populated, at about 1,700 people per km^2^; large areas of Luxembourg City are maintained as parks, forested areas, or sites of important heritage (particularly the [UNESCO](/wiki/UNESCO "UNESCO") sites), while there are also large tracts of farmland within the city limits.  

### Quarters of Luxembourg City {#Quarters_of_Luxembourg_City}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Quarters of Luxembourg City")\]  
Main article: [Quarters of Luxembourg City](/wiki/Quarters_of_Luxembourg_City "Quarters of Luxembourg City")

Luxembourg City is subdivided into twenty-four [quarters](/wiki/Quarter_(urban_subdivision) "Quarter (urban subdivision)") (French: *quartiers* ), which cover the commune in its entirety. The quarters generally correspond to the major neighbourhoods and suburbs of Luxembourg City, although a few of the historic districts, such as [Bonnevoie](/wiki/Bonnevoie "Bonnevoie"), are divided between two quarters.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^  

### Climate {#Climate}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Climate")\]

Luxembourg City has an [oceanic climate](/wiki/Oceanic_climate "Oceanic climate") ([Cfb](/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification "Köppen climate classification")), with moderate precipitation, cold to cool winters and warm summers. It is cloudy about two-thirds of the year.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^

### Local government {#Local_government}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Local government")\]  
See also: [Luxembourg communal council](/wiki/Luxembourg_communal_council "Luxembourg communal council") and [List of mayors of Luxembourg City](/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_Luxembourg_City "List of mayors of Luxembourg City")
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/Luxemb_City_Hotel_de_ville_01.jpg/220px-Luxemb_City_Hotel_de_ville_01.jpg)](/wiki/File:Luxemb_City_Hotel_de_ville_01.jpg) [Luxembourg City Hall](/wiki/Luxembourg_City_Hall "Luxembourg City Hall") is the heart of the communal administration, and hosts the offices of both the [communal council](/wiki/Luxembourg_communal_council "Luxembourg communal council") and the [mayor](/wiki/Mayor_of_Luxembourg_City "Mayor of Luxembourg City").

Under the [Luxembourgish constitution](/wiki/Constitution_of_Luxembourg "Constitution of Luxembourg"), local government is centred on the city's [communal council](/wiki/Luxembourg_communal_council "Luxembourg communal council"). Consisting of twenty-seven members (fixed since 1964), each elected every six years on the second Sunday of October and taking office on 1 January of the next year,^[\[19\]](#cite_note-21)^ the council is the largest of all communal councils in Luxembourg. The city is considered a stronghold of the [Democratic Party](/wiki/Democratic_Party_(Luxembourg) "Democratic Party (Luxembourg)") (DP),^[\[20\]](#cite_note-Cliff-hanger-22)^ which as provided its mayor without interruption since 1969 and is the second-largest party nationally. The Democratic Party is the largest party on the council, with ten councillors.^[\[21\]](#cite_note-23)^{#cite_ref-23}

The city's administration is headed by the [mayor](/wiki/Mayor_of_Luxembourg_City "Mayor of Luxembourg City"), who is the leader of the largest party on the communal council. After [Xavier Bettel](/wiki/Xavier_Bettel "Xavier Bettel") became Luxembourg's new prime minister on 4 December 2013, [Lydie Polfer](/wiki/Lydie_Polfer "Lydie Polfer") (DP), who had already been in office from 1982 to 1999, was sworn in as new mayor of Luxembourg on 17 December of the same year. Since the last elections the mayor leads the cabinet, the [College of Aldermen](/wiki/Coll%C3%A8ge_%C3%A9chevinal_(Luxembourg) "Collège échevinal (Luxembourg)"), in which the DP forms a coalition with the [CSV](/wiki/Christian_Social_People%27s_Party "Christian Social People's Party"). Unlike other cities in Luxembourg, which are limited to four [aldermen](/wiki/%C3%89chevin_(Luxembourg) "Échevin (Luxembourg)") at most, Luxembourg is given special dispensation to have six aldermen on its College.^[\[22\]](#cite_note-24)^{#cite_ref-24}  

### National government {#National_government}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: National government")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/28/Kirchberg_Luxembourg.jpg/220px-Kirchberg_Luxembourg.jpg)](/wiki/File:Kirchberg_Luxembourg.jpg) The Plateau de Kirchberg has many new buildings including those for the European Institutions.

Luxembourg City is the seat for the Luxembourg Government. The [Grand Ducal Family](/wiki/Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg "Grand Duke of Luxembourg") of Luxembourg lives at [Berg Castle](/wiki/Berg_Castle "Berg Castle") in [Colmar-Berg](/wiki/Colmar-Berg "Colmar-Berg").

For national elections to the Chamber of Deputies, the city is located in the [Centre](/wiki/Centre_(Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Luxembourg_constituency) "Centre (Chamber of Deputies of Luxembourg constituency)") constituency.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^  

### European institutions {#European_institutions}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: European institutions")\]

Luxembourg City is the seat of several institutions, agencies and bodies of the [European Union](/wiki/European_Union "European Union"), including the [Court of Justice of the European Union](/wiki/Court_of_Justice_of_the_European_Union "Court of Justice of the European Union"), the [European Commission](/wiki/European_Commission "European Commission"), the secretariat of the [European Parliament](/wiki/European_Parliament "European Parliament"), the [European Court of Auditors](/wiki/European_Court_of_Auditors "European Court of Auditors") and the [European Investment Bank](/wiki/European_Investment_Bank "European Investment Bank"). The majority of these institutions are located in the [Kirchberg](/wiki/Kirchberg,_Luxembourg "Kirchberg, Luxembourg") quarter, in the northeast of the city.^[\[23\]](#cite_note-25)^{#cite_ref-25}  

Culture {#Culture}
------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Culture")\]  
Main article: [Culture of Luxembourg](/wiki/Culture_of_Luxembourg "Culture of Luxembourg")
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Casino_Luxembourg_rue_Notre-Dame_01.jpg/150px-Casino_Luxembourg_rue_Notre-Dame_01.jpg)](/wiki/File:Casino_Luxembourg_rue_Notre-Dame_01.jpg) [Casino Luxembourg](/wiki/Casino_Luxembourg "Casino Luxembourg") is used for exhibitions of local art. [![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/ec/Luxemburg_Grand_Theatre_2.jpg/150px-Luxemburg_Grand_Theatre_2.jpg)](/wiki/File:Luxemburg_Grand_Theatre_2.jpg) [Grand Théâtre de Luxembourg](/wiki/Grand_Th%C3%A9%C3%A2tre_de_Luxembourg "Grand Théâtre de Luxembourg")

Despite the city's small size, it has several notable museums: the recently renovated [National Museum of History and Art](/wiki/National_Museum_of_History_and_Art "National Museum of History and Art") (MNHA), the [Luxembourg City History Museum](/wiki/Luxembourg_City_History_Museum "Luxembourg City History Museum"), the new [Grand Duke Jean Museum of Modern Art](/wiki/Grand_Duke_Jean_Museum_of_Modern_Art "Grand Duke Jean Museum of Modern Art") (Mudam) and [National Museum of Natural History](/wiki/National_Museum_of_Natural_History_(Luxembourg) "National Museum of Natural History (Luxembourg)") (NMHN).

Luxembourg was the first city to be named [European Capital of Culture](/wiki/European_Capital_of_Culture "European Capital of Culture") twice. The first time was in 1995. In 2007, along with the [Romanian](/wiki/Romania "Romania") city of [Sibiu](/wiki/Sibiu "Sibiu"), the European Capital of Culture^[\[24\]](#cite_note-26)^ was to be a cross-border area consisting of the Grand Duchy of Luxembourg, the Rheinland-Pfalz and Saarland in Germany, the Walloon Region and the German-speaking part of Belgium, and the [Lorraine](/wiki/Lorraine "Lorraine") area in [France](/wiki/France "France"). The event was an attempt to promote mobility and the exchange of ideas, crossing borders in all areas, physical, psychological, artistic and emotional.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^{#cite_ref-26}

Luxembourg City is also famed for its wide selection of restaurants and cuisines, including four Michelin starred establishments.^[\[25\]](#cite_note-27)^{#cite_ref-27}  

### UNESCO World Heritage Site {#UNESCO_World_Heritage_Site}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=12 "Edit section: UNESCO World Heritage Site")\]  
Main article: [Old City of Luxembourg](/wiki/Old_City_of_Luxembourg "Old City of Luxembourg")
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/LUX_%E2%80%93_Kanton_Luxemburg_%E2%80%94_Gemeinde_Luxembourg_%E2%80%94_Oberstadt_%E2%80%94_Chemin_de_la_Corniche_%28Bronzetafel_UNESCO-Welterbest%C3%A4tte%29_2024-01-27_Mattes.jpg/100px-LUX_%E2%80%93_Kanton_Luxemburg_%E2%80%94_Gemeinde_Luxembourg_%E2%80%94_Oberstadt_%E2%80%94_Chemin_de_la_Corniche_%28Bronzetafel_UNESCO-Welterbest%C3%A4tte%29_2024-01-27_Mattes.jpg)](/wiki/File:LUX_%E2%80%93_Kanton_Luxemburg_%E2%80%94_Gemeinde_Luxembourg_%E2%80%94_Oberstadt_%E2%80%94_Chemin_de_la_Corniche_(Bronzetafel_UNESCO-Welterbest%C3%A4tte)_2024-01-27_Mattes.jpg)

The city of Luxembourg is on the [UNESCO](/wiki/UNESCO "UNESCO") [World Heritage List](/wiki/World_Heritage_List "World Heritage List") as *City of Luxembourg: its Old Quarters and Fortifications* , on account of the historical importance of its fortifications.^[\[26\]](#cite_note-28)^ In addition to its two main theatres, the [Grand Théâtre de Luxembourg](/wiki/Grand_Th%C3%A9%C3%A2tre_de_Luxembourg "Grand Théâtre de Luxembourg") and the [Théâtre des Capucins](/wiki/Th%C3%A9%C3%A2tre_des_Capucins "Théâtre des Capucins"), there is a new concert hall, the [Philharmonie](/wiki/Philharmonie_Luxembourg "Philharmonie Luxembourg"), as well as a [conservatory](/wiki/Conservatoire_de_Luxembourg "Conservatoire de Luxembourg") with a large auditorium. Art galleries include the [Villa Vauban](/wiki/Villa_Vauban "Villa Vauban"), the [Casino Luxembourg](/wiki/Casino_Luxembourg "Casino Luxembourg") and [Am Tunnel](/wiki/Am_Tunnel "Am Tunnel").^[\[27\]](#cite_note-29)^{#cite_ref-29}

The site is located mainly in Ville Haute (Uewerstad).  

### Sport {#Sport}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=13 "Edit section: Sport")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Stade_de_Luxembourg%2C_Luxembourg_vs_Azebaidjan_2021-09-01_%28103%29.jpg/220px-Stade_de_Luxembourg%2C_Luxembourg_vs_Azebaidjan_2021-09-01_%28103%29.jpg)](/wiki/File:Stade_de_Luxembourg,_Luxembourg_vs_Azebaidjan_2021-09-01_(103).jpg) Stade de Luxembourg

The [ING](/wiki/ING_Group "ING Group") [Europe Marathon](/wiki/Europe_Marathon "Europe Marathon") has been contested annually in the capital since June 2006. It attracted 11,000 runners and over 100,000 spectators during the 2014 edition.

The [Luxembourg Open](/wiki/Luxembourg_Open "Luxembourg Open") is a tennis tournament held since 1991 in the capital. The tournament runs from 13 to 21 October. [BGL BNP Paribas](/wiki/BGL_BNP_Paribas "BGL BNP Paribas"), one of the more famous sponsors in the world of tennis, was the contracted title sponsor of the tournament until 2014.

The [Stade de Luxembourg](/wiki/Stade_de_Luxembourg "Stade de Luxembourg"), situated in [Gasperich](/wiki/Gasperich "Gasperich"), southern Luxembourg City, is the country's [national stadium](/wiki/National_stadium "National stadium") and largest sports venue in the country with a capacity of 9,386 for sporting events, including football and rugby union, and 15,000 for concerts.^[\[28\]](#cite_note-30)^ The largest [indoor venue](/wiki/Indoor_arena "Indoor arena") in the country is [d'Coque](/wiki/D%27Coque "D'Coque"), [Kirchberg](/wiki/Kirchberg,_Luxembourg "Kirchberg, Luxembourg"), north-eastern Luxembourg City, which has a capacity of 8,300. The arena is used for [basketball](/wiki/Basketball "Basketball"), [handball](/wiki/Team_handball "Team handball"), [gymnastics](/wiki/Gymnastics "Gymnastics"), and [volleyball](/wiki/Volleyball "Volleyball"), including the final of the [2007 Women's European Volleyball Championship](/wiki/2007_Women%27s_European_Volleyball_Championship "2007 Women's European Volleyball Championship"). D'Coque also includes an [Olympic-size swimming pool](/wiki/Olympic-size_swimming_pool "Olympic-size swimming pool").^[\[29\]](#cite_note-31)^{#cite_ref-31}

The two football clubs of the city of Luxembourg; [Racing FC Union Luxembourg](/wiki/Racing_FC_Union_Luxembourg "Racing FC Union Luxembourg") and [F.C. Luxembourg City](/wiki/FC_RM_Hamm_Benfica "FC RM Hamm Benfica"), play in the country's highest league, the [BGL Ligue](/wiki/Luxembourg_National_Division "Luxembourg National Division"), and second-tier, [Division of Honour](/wiki/Luxembourg_Division_of_Honour "Luxembourg Division of Honour"), respectively. The Stade de Luxembourg hosts the [Luxembourg national football team](/wiki/Luxembourg_national_football_team "Luxembourg national football team").

### Public transport {#Public_transport}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=17 "Edit section: Public transport")\]

Public transport in Luxembourg City has been free since 2020, including rail, bus and tram.^[\[30\]](#cite_note-32)^{#cite_ref-32}  

#### Rail {#Rail}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=18 "Edit section: Rail")\]

Luxembourg City is served by five rail stations operated by the state rail company, the [Société Nationale des Chemins de Fer Luxembourgeois](/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_Nationale_des_Chemins_de_Fer_Luxembourgeois "Société Nationale des Chemins de Fer Luxembourgeois") (CFL), including the principal station and terminus of all rail lines in the Grand Duchy, [Luxembourg station](/wiki/Luxembourg_railway_station "Luxembourg railway station"). Stations in Luxembourg City are served by domestic rail services operated by CFL, as well as international rail services, operated by CFL, and [German](/wiki/Deutsche_Bahn "Deutsche Bahn"), [Belgian](/wiki/National_Railway_Company_of_Belgium "National Railway Company of Belgium"), and [French](/wiki/SNCF "SNCF") service providers. Additionally, Luxembourg station is connected to the French [LGV Est](/wiki/LGV_Est "LGV Est") network, providing high-speed services on to Paris and [Strasbourg](/wiki/Strasbourg "Strasbourg"). Services to [Basel](/wiki/Basel "Basel") and [Zürich](/wiki/Z%C3%BCrich "Zürich") in Switzerland are available via two daily scheduled international trains.^[\[31\]](#cite_note-33)^{#cite_ref-33}  

#### Bus {#Bus}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=19 "Edit section: Bus")\]

Luxembourg City has a network of 40^[\[32\]](#cite_note-34)^ bus routes, operated by the municipal transport authority, *Autobus de la Ville de Luxembourg* (AVL), partly subcontracted to private bus companies. There is also a free bus service linking the Glacis to [Luxembourg station](/wiki/Luxembourg_railway_station "Luxembourg railway station"), the "Joker Line" for seniors, and a "City night network". A "Park \& Ride" scheme is operated by the city with five carparks connected to the bus network. In addition to AVL buses, [CFL](/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_Nationale_des_Chemins_de_Fer_Luxembourgeois "Société Nationale des Chemins de Fer Luxembourgeois") and RGTR operate regional buses to other locales in Luxembourg and nearby cities in Germany and France.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^{#cite_ref-34}  

#### Tram {#Tram}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=20 "Edit section: Tram")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Tram_in_Luxemburg_stad_in_2021_9.jpg/220px-Tram_in_Luxemburg_stad_in_2021_9.jpg)](/wiki/File:Tram_in_Luxemburg_stad_in_2021_9.jpg) Tram in Luxembourg City

Between 1875 and 1964, the city was covered by an extensive tram network. In December 2017, [trams](/wiki/Trams_in_Luxembourg "Trams in Luxembourg") were reintroduced to the capital, with the phased opening of a new line, which currently runs between [Kirchberg](/wiki/Kirchberg,_Luxembourg "Kirchberg, Luxembourg") and [Gasperich](/wiki/Gasperich "Gasperich"), via the city centre.^[\[33\]](#cite_note-35)^^[\[34\]](#cite_note-36)^ An extension to [Luxembourg Airport](/wiki/Luxembourg_Airport "Luxembourg Airport") is expected to be operational on 2 March 2025.^[\[35\]](#cite_note-37)^ The network is expected to have 4 lines by 2035, with future lines currently in the planning stages.^[\[36\]](#cite_note-38)^{#cite_ref-38}  

### Air {#Air}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_City&action=edit&section=21 "Edit section: Air")\]

Luxembourg City is served by the only international airport in the country: [Luxembourg Airport](/wiki/Luxembourg_Airport "Luxembourg Airport") (codes: [IATA](/wiki/IATA "IATA"): LUX, [ICAO](/wiki/ICAO "ICAO"): ELLX). Accessibility to the airport, situated in the commune of [Sandweiler](/wiki/Sandweiler "Sandweiler"), 6 kilometres (3.7 miles) from the city centre, is provided via the municipal bus network, with a tram connection due to be completed on 2 March 2025.^[\[37\]](#cite_note-39)^ The airport is the principal hub for Luxembourg's [flag carrier](/wiki/Flag_carrier "Flag carrier"), [Luxair](/wiki/Luxair "Luxair"), and one of the world's largest cargo airlines, [Cargolux](/wiki/Cargolux "Cargolux").^[\[38\]](#cite_note-40)^{#cite_ref-40}
