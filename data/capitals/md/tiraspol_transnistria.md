**Tiraspol** ([Russian](/wiki/Russian_language "Russian language"): Тирасполь,
pronounced [\[tʲɪˈraspəlʲ\]](/wiki/Help:IPA/Russian "Help:IPA/Russian"); [Romanian](/wiki/Romanian_language "Romanian language"): *Tiraspol* , [\[tɪˈrɑspol\]](/wiki/Help:IPA/Romanian "Help:IPA/Romanian"), [Moldovan Cyrillic](/wiki/Moldovan_Cyrillic "Moldovan Cyrillic"): Тираспол, also *Tirișpolea*;^[\[4\]](#cite_note-5)^^[\[5\]](#cite_note-6)^ [Ukrainian](/wiki/Ukrainian_language "Ukrainian language"): Тирасполь, [romanized](/wiki/Romanization_of_Ukrainian "Romanization of Ukrainian"): *Tyraspol'*) is the capital and largest city of [Transnistria](/wiki/Transnistria "Transnistria"), a [breakaway state](/wiki/Breakaway_state "Breakaway state") of [Moldova](/wiki/Moldova "Moldova"), where it is the third-largest city. The city is located on the eastern bank of the [Dniester](/wiki/Dniester "Dniester") River. Tiraspol is a regional hub of culture, economy, tourism, and [light industry](/wiki/Light_industry "Light industry"), such as furniture and electrical goods production.{#cite_ref-6}

The modern city of Tiraspol was founded by the Russian [generalissimo](/wiki/Generalissimo "Generalissimo") [Alexander Suvorov](/wiki/Alexander_Suvorov "Alexander Suvorov") in 1792, although the area had been inhabited for thousands of years by varying ethnic groups.^[\[6\]](#cite_note-7)^ The city celebrates its anniversary every year on 14 October.^[\[7\]](#cite_note-8)^{#cite_ref-8}

### Classical and medieval history {#Classical_and_medieval_history}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Tiraspol&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Classical and medieval history")\]

[Tyras](/wiki/Tyras "Tyras") (Τύρας), also spelled *Tiras* , was a [colony](/wiki/Greek_colonisation "Greek colonisation") of the Greek city [Miletus](/wiki/Miletus "Miletus"), probably founded about 600 BC, situated some 10 kilometres (6 miles) from the mouth of the Tiras River (Dniester). Of no great importance in early times, in the second century BC it fell under the dominion of [indigenous](/wiki/Indigenous_peoples "Indigenous peoples") kings whose names appear on its coins. It was destroyed by the [Thracian](/wiki/Thracians "Thracians") [Getae](/wiki/Getae "Getae") about 50 BC.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^

In 56 AD, the [Romans](/wiki/Roman_Republic "Roman Republic") restored the city and made it part of the [colonial province](/wiki/Roman_province "Roman province") of [Lower Moesia](/wiki/Lower_Moesia "Lower Moesia").^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^ A series of its coins exist that feature heads of [Roman emperors](/wiki/Roman_emperors "Roman emperors") from [Domitian](/wiki/Domitian "Domitian") to [Severus Alexander](/wiki/Severus_Alexander "Severus Alexander"). Soon after the time of the latter, the city was destroyed again, this time by the invasion of the [Goths](/wiki/Goths "Goths"). Its government was in the hands of five [archons](/wiki/Archon "Archon"), a [senate](/wiki/Senate "Senate"), a [popular assembly](/wiki/Ecclesia_(ancient_Athens) "Ecclesia (ancient Athens)"), and a [registrar](/wiki/Civil_registration "Civil registration"). The images on its coins from this period suggest a trade in wheat, wine, and fish. The few [inscriptions](/wiki/Epigraphy "Epigraphy") extant are mostly concerned with trade.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^

Such ancient [archeological](/wiki/Archeological "Archeological") remains are scanty, as the city site was built over by the great medieval fortress of Monocastro or [Akkerman](/wiki/Akkerman "Akkerman").^[\[9\]](#cite_note-10)^ In the [Late Middle Ages](/wiki/Late_Middle_Ages "Late Middle Ages"), the area around of modern Tiraspol became part of the [Grand Duchy of Lithuania](/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Lithuania "Grand Duchy of Lithuania"). The Lithuanians, unable to settle their vast state on their own, allowed [Moldavian](/wiki/Romanians "Romanians") settlement in the area.^[\[10\]](#cite_note-FOOTNOTECucerescuRosca2015187-11)^{#cite_ref-FOOTNOTECucerescuRosca2015187_11-0}

The [Russian Empire](/wiki/Russian_Empire "Russian Empire") conquered its way to the [Dniester](/wiki/Dniester "Dniester") River, taking territory from the [Ottoman Empire](/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire"). In 1792 the [Russian army](/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Army "Imperial Russian Army") built fortifications to guard the western border near a Moldavian village named [Sucleia](/wiki/Sucleia "Sucleia"). Field Marshal [Alexander Suvorov](/wiki/Alexander_Suvorov "Alexander Suvorov") is considered the founder of modern Tiraspol; [his statue](/wiki/Suvorov_Monument_(Tiraspol) "Suvorov Monument (Tiraspol)") is the city's most distinctive landmark.^[\[11\]](#cite_note-12)^ The city took its name from *[Tyras](/wiki/Tyras "Tyras")* , the [Greek](/wiki/Greeks "Greeks") name of the Dniester River on which it stands. It was granted city rights in 1795.^[\[2\]](#cite_note-FOOTNOTECucerescuRosca2015188-3)^{#cite_ref-FOOTNOTECucerescuRosca2015188_3-1}

In 1828, the Russian government established a customs house in Tiraspol to try to suppress [smuggling](/wiki/Smuggling "Smuggling"). The customs house was subordinated to the chief of the [Odesa](/wiki/Odesa "Odesa") customs region. It began operations with 14 employees. They inspected shipments of bread, paper, oil, wine, sugar, fruits and other goods.

In December 1917, Tiraspol hosted a congress of Transnistrian Moldovans, at which they wished to unite the city with [Bessarabia](/wiki/Bessarabia "Bessarabia").^[\[12\]](#cite_note-FOOTNOTECucerescuRosca2015189-13)^{#cite_ref-FOOTNOTECucerescuRosca2015189_13-0}  

### Soviet Tiraspol {#Soviet_Tiraspol}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Tiraspol&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Soviet Tiraspol")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Tiraspol_3.JPG/220px-Tiraspol_3.JPG)](/wiki/File:Tiraspol_3.JPG) Tiraspol in 1941

After the [Russian Revolution](/wiki/Russian_Revolution "Russian Revolution"), the [Moldavian Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic](/wiki/Moldavian_Autonomous_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Moldavian Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic") was created in [Ukraine](/wiki/Ukraine "Ukraine") in 1924, with [Balta](/wiki/Balta,_Ukraine "Balta, Ukraine") as its capital.^[\[12\]](#cite_note-FOOTNOTECucerescuRosca2015189-13)^ The republic had [Romanian](/wiki/Romanian_language "Romanian language"), [Ukrainian](/wiki/Ukrainian_language "Ukrainian language") and Russian as its official languages. Its capital was moved in 1929 to Tiraspol, which remained the capital of the Moldavian ASSR until 1940.^[\[13\]](#cite_note-14)^^[\[12\]](#cite_note-FOOTNOTECucerescuRosca2015189-13)^{#cite_ref-FOOTNOTECucerescuRosca2015189_13-2}

In 1940, following the secret provisions of the [Molotov--Ribbentrop Pact](/wiki/Molotov%E2%80%93Ribbentrop_Pact "Molotov–Ribbentrop Pact"), the USSR forced Romania to cede [Bessarabia](/wiki/Bessarabia "Bessarabia"). It integrated Tiraspol, until then part of the [Moldavian ASSR](/wiki/Moldavian_ASSR "Moldavian ASSR"), into the newly formed [Moldavian SSR](/wiki/Moldavian_SSR "Moldavian SSR"). On 7 August 1941, following the [Axis invasion of the Soviet Union](/wiki/Operation_Barbarossa "Operation Barbarossa"), the city was taken over by [Romanian troops](/wiki/Romania_during_World_War_II "Romania during World War II"). Later that month, on 19 August, the [Tiraspol Agreement](/wiki/Tiraspol_Agreement "Tiraspol Agreement") establishing the [Transnistria Governorate](/wiki/Transnistria_Governorate "Transnistria Governorate") was signed. During the occupation, Tiraspol was under Romanian administration. During that period almost all of its Jewish population died: they were slain *in situ* or deported to German Nazi [death camps](/wiki/Death_camp "Death camp"), and killed there.
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Tiraspoljet.JPG/220px-Tiraspoljet.JPG)](/wiki/File:Tiraspoljet.JPG) Soviet [MiG-19](/wiki/Mikoyan-Gurevich_MiG-19 "Mikoyan-Gurevich MiG-19") monument

In 1941, before the occupation, the newspaper *[Dnestrovskaya Pravda](/wiki/Dnestrovskaya_Pravda "Dnestrovskaya Pravda")* was founded by the Tiraspol City Council of popular deputies. This is the oldest periodical publication in the region. On 12 April 1944, the city was retaken by the [Red Army](/wiki/Red_Army "Red Army") and became again part of the Moldavian SSR.  

### Post-secession {#Post-secession}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Tiraspol&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Post-secession")\]

On 27 January 1990, the citizens in Tiraspol passed a [referendum](/wiki/Referendum "Referendum") declaring the city as an [independent territory](/wiki/Independence "Independence").^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^ The nearby city of [Bendery](/wiki/Bender,_Moldova "Bender, Moldova") also declared its independence from [Moldova](/wiki/Moldova "Moldova"). As the [Russian-speaking](/wiki/Russian_language "Russian language") independence movement gained momentum, some local governments banded together to resist pressure from the [Moldovan](/wiki/Moldova "Moldova") government for nationalization.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Calle_Tiraspol.jpg/220px-Calle_Tiraspol.jpg)](/wiki/File:Calle_Tiraspol.jpg) A trolleybus in Tiraspol painted in the colours of the [Transnistrian flag](/wiki/Flag_of_Transnistria "Flag of Transnistria")

On 2 September 1990, Tiraspol was proclaimed the capital of the new [Pridnestrovian Moldavian Soviet Socialist Republic](/wiki/Pridnestrovian_Moldavian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Pridnestrovian Moldavian Soviet Socialist Republic"). The new republic was not officially recognized by Soviet authorities; however, it received support from some important Soviet leaders, such as [Anatoly Lukyanov](/wiki/Anatoly_Lukyanov "Anatoly Lukyanov").^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^ After the dissolution of the Soviet Union, the territory east of the Dniester River declared independence as the [Pridnestrovian Moldavian Republic](/wiki/Transnistria "Transnistria") (PMR), with Tiraspol as its capital. It was not recognized by the international community.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Helden-Denkmal_Tiraspol_2012.jpg/220px-Helden-Denkmal_Tiraspol_2012.jpg)](/wiki/File:Helden-Denkmal_Tiraspol_2012.jpg) Heroes memorial in Tiraspol (2012)

On 1 July 2005, the Lucian Blaga Lyceum, a high school with Romanian as its language of instruction, was registered as a Transnistrian non-governmental establishment. The registration of [six Romanian language schools](/wiki/Romanian-language_schools_in_Transnistria "Romanian-language schools in Transnistria") has been the subject of negotiations with the government since 2000.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^ The tension increased in the summer of 2004, when the Transnistrian authorities forcibly closed the schools that taught using the [Latin script](/wiki/Latin_script "Latin script"). According to the official PMR view, this is considered as [Romanian](/wiki/Romanian_language "Romanian language"). "[Moldovan](/wiki/Moldovan_language "Moldovan language")", written in the [Cyrillic script](/wiki/Cyrillic_script "Cyrillic script"), is one of the three official languages in the PMR; Romanian is not. Some economic measures and counter-measures were taken on both banks of the Dniester.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^

Tensions have been seen in [terrorist](/wiki/Terrorist "Terrorist") incidents. On 6 July 2006, an explosion, believed to be caused by a bomb, killed at least eight people in a minibus.^[\[14\]](#cite_note-15)^ Later on 13 August, a grenade exploded in a [trolleybus](/wiki/Trolleybus "Trolleybus"), killing two and injuring ten.^[\[15\]](#cite_note-16)^^[\[16\]](#cite_note-17)^^[\[17\]](#cite_note-18)^{#cite_ref-18}

On 25 April 2022, during the [Russian invasion of Ukraine](/wiki/Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine "Russian invasion of Ukraine"), [several explosions](/wiki/2022_Transnistria_attacks "2022 Transnistria attacks") were reported near the [Ministry of State Security (Transnistria)](/wiki/Ministry_of_State_Security_(Transnistria) "Ministry of State Security (Transnistria)") in Tiraspol. Firefighters were on the scene but there were no casualties.^[\[18\]](#cite_note-19)^{#cite_ref-19}

On 17 March 2024, Transnistrian official press released a video allegedly^[\[19\]](#cite_note-2024drone1-20)^ showing a [Mil Mi-8](/wiki/Mil_Mi-8 "Mil Mi-8") helicopter in poor condition and likely not in use,^[\[20\]](#cite_note-2024drone2-21)^ not having been moved in over 13 years at the moment of the explosion,^[\[19\]](#cite_note-2024drone1-20)^ in a military unit in Tiraspol being attacked and destroyed by a kamikaze drone. No victims were reported.^[\[20\]](#cite_note-2024drone2-21)^ Transnistria claimed the drone had been launched from Ukraine from the direction of [Odesa](/wiki/Odesa "Odesa"), more precisely from a bridge located 5--6 kilometres (3.1--3.7 miles) from the nearest border crossing between Transnistria and Ukraine. Ukraine immediately denied having anything to do with the incident.^[\[19\]](#cite_note-2024drone1-20)^ On the day of the incident, the [Bureau for Reintegration of the Republic of Moldova](/w/index.php?title=Bureau_for_Reintegration_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova&action=edit&redlink=1 "Bureau for Reintegration of the Republic of Moldova (page does not exist)") called it an "attempt to provoke panic and fear in the region".^[\[20\]](#cite_note-2024drone2-21)^ Later, on 25 March, the bureau confirmed the explosion of the helicopter was not caused by a drone attack but by "other factors" and that the video contained "obvious elements of video montage". In the video, the drone disappears shortly before the explosion below the helicopter occurs. Reportedly, explosives placed under the helicopter were detonated remotely instead.^[\[19\]](#cite_note-2024drone1-20)^{#cite_ref-2024drone1_20-3}

On 8 January 2025, during the [2025 Moldovan energy crisis](/wiki/2025_Moldovan_energy_crisis "2025 Moldovan energy crisis"), a woman in Tiraspol died from [carbon monoxide poisoning](/wiki/Carbon_monoxide_poisoning "Carbon monoxide poisoning") after using a gas water heater to shower without having a [chimney](/wiki/Chimney "Chimney").^[\[21\]](#cite_note-22)^{#cite_ref-22}  

Geography and climate {#Geography_and_climate}
----------------------------------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Tiraspol&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Geography and climate")\]

Tiraspol features a [humid continental climate](/wiki/Humid_continental_climate "Humid continental climate") that closely borders an [oceanic climate](/wiki/Oceanic_climate "Oceanic climate") and has transitional features of the [humid subtropical climate](/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate "Humid subtropical climate") due to its warm summers. Summers are mild, with average monthly temperatures at around 21 °C (70 °F) in July and August. Winters are cold, with average temperatures in the coldest month (January) at −2.7 °C (27 °F). Precipitation is relatively evenly spread throughout the year, though there is a noticeable increase in monthly precipitation in June and July. Tiraspol on average sees nearly 500 mm (20 in) of precipitation per year.

### Religion {#Religion}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Tiraspol&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: Religion")\]

The Latin Catholic minority is served by the [Roman Catholic Diocese of Chișinău](/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u "Roman Catholic Diocese of Chișinău").

In the fourteenth century, Tiraspol was the see city of the diocese of Kherson; in 1848, a [Roman Catholic Diocese of Tiraspol](/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Tiraspol "Roman Catholic Diocese of Tiraspol") was erected with its see in Saratov. Since 1993, it did not cover any Moldovan territory, not even Tiraspol. The diocese was suppressed in 2002.

[The statue of Alexander Suvorov](/wiki/Suvorov_Monument_(Tiraspol) "Suvorov Monument (Tiraspol)") was erected in the central square in 1979 in commemoration of his 250th anniversary. In front of the Transnistrian Government building there is a statue of [Vladimir Lenin](/wiki/Vladimir_Lenin "Vladimir Lenin"). On the opposite side of the central square, a monument plaza features a Soviet [T-34](/wiki/T-34 "T-34") tank, commemorating the Soviet victory in [World War II](/wiki/World_War_II "World War II"), an eternal flame to those who fell defending the city in 1941 and liberating it in 1944, as well as several monuments dedicated to more recent conflicts, including the [Soviet--Afghan War](/wiki/Soviet%E2%80%93Afghan_War "Soviet–Afghan War") and the [War of Transnistria](/wiki/War_of_Transnistria "War of Transnistria").  

Sport {#Sport}
--------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Tiraspol&action=edit&section=12 "Edit section: Sport")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Tiraspol_Sheriff_Stadion.JPG/220px-Tiraspol_Sheriff_Stadion.JPG)](/wiki/File:Tiraspol_Sheriff_Stadion.JPG) [Sheriff Arena](/wiki/Sheriff_Arena "Sheriff Arena")

The two main football clubs are [Sheriff Tiraspol](/wiki/FC_Sheriff_Tiraspol "FC Sheriff Tiraspol") and [FC Tiraspol](/wiki/FC_Tiraspol "FC Tiraspol"). Sheriff is the most successful Moldovan football club of recent history, winning 14 league titles since the 2000--2001 season, and 6 [Moldovan Cups](/wiki/Moldovan_Cup "Moldovan Cup"). The team gained world notoriety for their last-minute 2--1 victory during the [2021--22 UEFA Champions League](/wiki/2021%E2%80%9322_UEFA_Champions_League "2021–22 UEFA Champions League") edition against later tournament winners Real Madrid on 28 September 2021. A third club, [CS Tiligul-Tiras Tiraspol](/wiki/CS_Tiligul-Tiras_Tiraspol "CS Tiligul-Tiras Tiraspol"), withdrew from competition prior to the 2009--2010 season. Tiraspol is home to the [Sheriff Stadium](/wiki/Sheriff_Stadium "Sheriff Stadium"), the largest capacity stadium in the region, with a capacity of 14,300 seats.
