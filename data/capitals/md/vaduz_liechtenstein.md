**Vaduz** (
German pronunciation: [\[faˈdʊt͡s\]](/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German "Help:IPA/Standard German") [](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/transcoded/2/24/Vaduz.wav/Vaduz.wav.mp3 "Play audio")^[ⓘ](/wiki/File:Vaduz.wav "File:Vaduz.wav")^ or [\[faˈduːt͡s\]](/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German "Help:IPA/Standard German") [](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/transcoded/a/a9/De-Vaduz.ogg/De-Vaduz.ogg.mp3 "Play audio")^[ⓘ](/wiki/File:De-Vaduz.ogg "File:De-Vaduz.ogg")^;^[\[2\]](#cite_note-2)^ [High Alemannic](/wiki/High_Alemannic_German "High Alemannic German") pronunciation: \[faˈdot͡s\])^[\[3\]](#cite_note-Hans_Stricker_1999-3)^ is the capital of [Liechtenstein](/wiki/Liechtenstein "Liechtenstein") and also the seat of the [national parliament](/wiki/Landtag_of_Liechtenstein "Landtag of Liechtenstein"). The village, which is located along the [Rhine](/wiki/Rhine "Rhine"), has 5,696 residents.^[\[1\]](#cite_note-llv.li-1)^ The most prominent landmark of Vaduz is [Vaduz Castle](/wiki/Vaduz_Castle "Vaduz Castle"), perched atop a steep hill overlooking the village. It is home to the reigning prince of Liechtenstein and the [Liechtenstein princely family](/wiki/Princely_Family_of_Liechtenstein "Princely Family of Liechtenstein"). The village's distinctive architecture is also displayed in landmarks such as the [Cathedral of St. Florin](/wiki/Vaduz_Cathedral "Vaduz Cathedral"), Government House, Village Hall, the National Art Gallery, as well as the National Museum. Although Vaduz is the best-known village in the principality internationally, it is not the largest; neighbouring [Schaan](/wiki/Schaan "Schaan") has a larger population.{#cite_ref-llv.li_1-1}  

Etymology {#Etymology}
----------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Vaduz&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Etymology")\]

The name *Vaduz* had been first recorded as *de Faduzes* . The name of the settlement, like most other towns in the [Rhine Valley](/wiki/Rhine_Valley "Rhine Valley") region, is of [Romance](/wiki/Romance_languages "Romance languages") origin. The name can be traced back to Old [Rhaeto-Romance](/wiki/Rhaeto-Romance_languages "Rhaeto-Romance languages") root *auadutg* '[aqueduct](/wiki/Aqueduct_(water_supply) "Aqueduct (water supply)")', which in turn evolved from the [Latin](/wiki/Latin "Latin") *aquaeductus*.^[\[3\]](#cite_note-Hans_Stricker_1999-3)^{#cite_ref-Hans_Stricker_1999_3-1}  

History {#History}
------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Vaduz&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: History")\]  
See also: [County of Vaduz](/wiki/County_of_Vaduz "County of Vaduz")

Vaduz is mentioned in historic 12th-century manuscripts as *Faduzes* . In 1322 a mention of the [castle](/wiki/Vaduz_Castle "Vaduz Castle") is made, which was sacked by the [Swiss](/wiki/Switzerland "Switzerland") in 1499 during the [Swabian War](/wiki/Swabian_War "Swabian War"). The entire village was also destroyed.^[\[4\]](#cite_note-4)^{#cite_ref-4}

In the 17th century the [Liechtenstein family](/wiki/Princely_Family_of_Liechtenstein "Princely Family of Liechtenstein") was seeking a seat in the Imperial diet, the *[Reichstag](/wiki/Reichstag_(Holy_Roman_Empire) "Reichstag (Holy Roman Empire)")* . However, since they did not hold any territory that was directly under the Imperial throne, they were unable to meet the primary requirement to qualify.^[\[5\]](#cite_note-5)^{#cite_ref-5}

The family yearned for the added power a seat in the Imperial government would bring, and therefore sought to acquire lands that would be *[reichsunmittelbar](/wiki/Reichsunmittelbar "Reichsunmittelbar")* , or held directly from the [Holy Roman Emperor](/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor "Holy Roman Emperor") himself, without any intermediate feudal patronage. After some time, the family was able to arrange the purchase of the minuscule [*Herrschaft* ("Lordship") of](/wiki/Lordship_of_Schellenberg "Lordship of Schellenberg") [Schellenberg](/wiki/Schellenberg "Schellenberg") and the [countship of Vaduz](/wiki/County_of_Vaduz "County of Vaduz") (in 1699 and 1712, respectively) from the Hohenems. Tiny Schellenberg and Vaduz possessed exactly the political status required: no feudal lord other than the Emperor.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^

Thereby, on 23 January 1719, after the purchase had been duly made, [Charles VI, Holy Roman Emperor](/wiki/Charles_VI,_Holy_Roman_Emperor "Charles VI, Holy Roman Emperor"), decreed Vaduz and Schellenberg were united, and raised to the dignity of *Fürstentum* (*[principality](/wiki/Principality "Principality")* ) with the name "Liechtenstein" in honour of "\[his\] true servant, [Anton Florian of Liechtenstein](/wiki/Anton_Florian_of_Liechtenstein "Anton Florian of Liechtenstein")". It is on this date that Liechtenstein became a sovereign member state of the [Holy Roman Empire](/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire "Holy Roman Empire"). As testimony to the pure political expediency of the purchases, the Princes of Liechtenstein did not set foot in their new principality for over 120 years.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^  

Politics {#Politics}
--------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Vaduz&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Politics")\]  
See also: [List of mayors of Vaduz](/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_Vaduz "List of mayors of Vaduz")

Vaduz is located in the [Oberland](/wiki/Oberland_(electoral_district) "Oberland (electoral district)") electoral district, which has fifteen seats in the [Landtag of Liechtenstein](/wiki/Landtag_of_Liechtenstein "Landtag of Liechtenstein"). Since the introduction of Liechtenstein municipal law of 1864, Vaduz has been locally administered by a mayor and municipal council. Until 1941, this consisted of the mayor, the municipal treasurer, and seven other councillors.^[\[6\]](#cite_note-:02-6)^{#cite_ref-:02_6-0}

In 1974, the municipal law was revised which extended the term of the mayor and council to four years and increased the seats of Vaduz's municipal council to twelve. The system to elect the municipal council was changed to use a [open list](/wiki/Open_list "Open list") [proportional representation](/wiki/Proportional_representation "Proportional representation") system. In 1976, Vaduz replaced [universal male suffrage](/wiki/Universal_male_suffrage "Universal male suffrage") with [universal suffrage](/wiki/Universal_suffrage "Universal suffrage").^[\[6\]](#cite_note-:02-6)^ Universal suffrage was not introduced to Liechtenstein on a national level until 1984.^[\[7\]](#cite_note-7)^{#cite_ref-7}

The incumbent mayor is [Florian Meier](/wiki/Florian_Meier "Florian Meier"), elected in the [2024 Vaduz mayoral by-election](/wiki/2024_Vaduz_mayoral_by-election "2024 Vaduz mayoral by-election").^[\[8\]](#cite_note-:LV-8)^{#cite_ref-:LV_8-0}

### Climate {#Climate}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Vaduz&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Climate")\]

Vaduz features an [oceanic climate](/wiki/Oceanic_climate "Oceanic climate") with warm summers and chilly winters. [Köppen-Geiger climate classification system](/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen-Geiger_climate_classification_system "Köppen-Geiger climate classification system") classifies its climate as Marine West Coast Climate (Cfb).^[\[9\]](#cite_note-9)^ The village experiences a noticeable increase in precipitation during the summer, but in general all twelve months see some precipitation. Vaduz receives, on average, approximately 900 mm (35 in) of precipitation per year. Vaduz's warmest month, July, sees average high temperatures reach 25 °C (77 °F) while average low temperatures are about 14 °C (57 °F). The village's coldest month, January, sees average highs of 3 °C (37 °F) and average lows of −3 °C (27 °F).{#cite_ref-9}

Demographics {#Demographics}
----------------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Vaduz&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Demographics")\]

As of 2019, 5,696 people lived in Vaduz.^[\[1\]](#cite_note-llv.li-1)^ Foreigners resident in the village make up 42% of the population.^[\[14\]](#cite_note-14)^ With 67% the population is predominantly [Roman Catholic](/wiki/Roman_Catholicism "Roman Catholicism"), while the percentage of Catholics is significantly higher among residents with Liechtenstein nationality (81%) than among foreigner residents (47%). The largest minority religions in the village are [Protestantism](/wiki/Protestantism "Protestantism") (10%) and [Islam](/wiki/Islam "Islam") (8%).^[\[15\]](#cite_note-religion_2015-15)^{#cite_ref-religion_2015_15-0}

Culture {#Culture}
------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Vaduz&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Culture")\]

The National Art Gallery as well as the National Museum are located in Vaduz. The art gallery ([Kunstmuseum Liechtenstein](/wiki/Kunstmuseum_Liechtenstein "Kunstmuseum Liechtenstein")) is a museum of modern and contemporary art, and also shows displays from the private princely [Liechtenstein Collection](/wiki/Liechtenstein_Collection "Liechtenstein Collection"), the main public display of which is in [Vienna](/wiki/Vienna "Vienna"). The building is an architectural landmark built by the Swiss architects Morger, Degelo and Kerez. It was completed in November 2000 and forms a "black box" of tinted concrete and black basalt stone. The museum collection is also the national art collection of Liechtenstein. The [Liechtenstein National Museum](/wiki/Liechtenstein_National_Museum "Liechtenstein National Museum") is showing a permanent exhibition on the cultural and natural history of Liechtenstein as well as special exhibitions. There are also the [Postage Stamp Museum](/wiki/Postal_Museum_(Liechtenstein) "Postal Museum (Liechtenstein)") and a Ski Museum. Vaduz has a kind of folksong that has been greatly influenced by Switzerland, known as *Köpugeäng*.  

Economy and transport {#Economy_and_transport}
----------------------------------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Vaduz&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: Economy and transport")\]

Vaduz is one of the few capital cities in the world to not have an airport. The closest major airport is [Zurich Airport](/wiki/Zurich_Airport "Zurich Airport"), and the closest minor airport being the [St. Gallen--Altenrhein Airport](/wiki/St._Gallen%E2%80%93Altenrhein_Airport "St. Gallen–Altenrhein Airport"). [Friedrichshafen Airport](/wiki/Friedrichshafen_Airport "Friedrichshafen Airport") also provides access to Vaduz. By car, Vaduz is directly accessible via the [A13](/wiki/A13_motorway_(Switzerland) "A13 motorway (Switzerland)") motorway in Switzerland, or via the [A14](/wiki/A14_motorway_(Austria) "A14 motorway (Austria)") motorway in Austria.^[\[16\]](#cite_note-16)^ Vaduz is connected to Switzerland over the Rhine river by the [Vaduz--Sevelen footbridge](/w/index.php?title=Vaduz%E2%80%93Sevelen_bridge&action=edit&redlink=1 "Vaduz–Sevelen bridge (page does not exist)") \[[de](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alte_Rheinbr%C3%BCcke_Vaduz%E2%80%93Sevelen "de:Alte Rheinbrücke Vaduz–Sevelen")\], or the Werdenberger-Binnenkanal bridge for motor vehicles, which was opened in 1975.^[\[6\]](#cite_note-:02-6)^ Buses can be taken from [Buchs, St. Gallen](/wiki/Buchs,_St._Gallen "Buchs, St. Gallen"), [Sevelen](/wiki/Sevelen "Sevelen") and [Feldkirch](/wiki/Feldkirch,_Vorarlberg "Feldkirch, Vorarlberg") into Vaduz. These buses typically run every 20 to 40 minutes and are operated by [Liechtenstein Bus](/wiki/Liechtenstein_Bus "Liechtenstein Bus").^[\[17\]](#cite_note-17)^{#cite_ref-17}

[Schaan-Vaduz railway station](/wiki/Schaan-Vaduz_railway_station "Schaan-Vaduz railway station"), located in Schaan, is the closest railway station to Vaduz. The station is situated on the [Feldkirch--Buchs railway](/wiki/Feldkirch%E2%80%93Buchs_railway "Feldkirch–Buchs railway"), [Liechtenstein's only railway line](/wiki/Rail_transport_in_Liechtenstein "Rail transport in Liechtenstein"), and is served by the S2 of the [Vorarlberg S-Bahn](/wiki/Vorarlberg_S-Bahn "Vorarlberg S-Bahn"), which is operated by [Austrian Federal Railways](/wiki/Austrian_Federal_Railways "Austrian Federal Railways") (ÖBB). It was opened on 24 October 1872 by agreement with [Austria-Hungary](/wiki/Austria-Hungary "Austria-Hungary") and Switzerland. There have been attempts to expand the rail network to connect with Vaduz, though these have been unsuccessful.^[\[18\]](#cite_note-18)^{#cite_ref-18}  

Education {#Education}
----------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Vaduz&action=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Education")\]

Vaduz has two primary schools: Äule Primary School, near the Vaduzer-Saal;^[\[19\]](#cite_note-Aule-19)^ and Ebenholz Primary School,^[\[20\]](#cite_note-Ebenholz-20)^ near the [University of Liechtenstein](/wiki/University_of_Liechtenstein "University of Liechtenstein"), which is also located in the village. Both schools have the same secretariat and administration.^[\[19\]](#cite_note-Aule-19)^ The school assignments of children are largely determined by their street addresses.^[\[20\]](#cite_note-Ebenholz-20)^ There are four kindergarten sites, of the Kindergarten Bartlegrosch, in Vaduz.^[\[21\]](#cite_note-21)^{#cite_ref-21}

[Realschule](/wiki/Realschule "Realschule") Vaduz and [Oberschule](/wiki/Oberschule "Oberschule") Vaduz are in the [Schulzentrum Mühleholz II](/w/index.php?title=Schulzentrum_M%C3%BChleholz_II&action=edit&redlink=1 "Schulzentrum Mühleholz II (page does not exist)") in Vaduz. [Liechtensteinisches Gymnasium](/wiki/Liechtensteinisches_Gymnasium "Liechtensteinisches Gymnasium") is also located in Vaduz. Realschule Schaan and Sportschule Liechtenstein are in nearby Schaan.^[\[22\]](#cite_note-22)^{#cite_ref-22}
