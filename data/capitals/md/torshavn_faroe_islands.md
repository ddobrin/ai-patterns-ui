**Tórshavn** (
Faroese: [\[ˈtʰɔu̯ʂhau̯n\]](/wiki/Help:IPA "Help:IPA") [](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/transcoded/8/8f/Fo-Torshavn.ogg/Fo-Torshavn.ogg.mp3 "Play audio")^[ⓘ](/wiki/File:Fo-Torshavn.ogg "File:Fo-Torshavn.ogg")^;  '[Thor](/wiki/Thor "Thor")'s harbour'; [Danish](/wiki/Danish_language "Danish language"): *Thorshavn* ), usually locally referred to as simply **Havn** , is the capital and largest city of the [Faroe Islands](/wiki/Faroe_Islands "Faroe Islands"). It is located in the southern part on the east coast of [Streymoy](/wiki/Streymoy "Streymoy"). To the northwest of the city lies the 347-meter-high (1,138 ft) mountain [Húsareyn](/wiki/H%C3%BAsareyn "Húsareyn"), and to the southwest, the 350-meter-high (1,150 ft) Kirkjubøreyn. They are separated by the Sandá River. The city itself has a population of 14,099 (2024), and the greater urban area has a population of 23,194, including the suburbs of [Hoyvík](/wiki/Hoyv%C3%ADk "Hoyvík") and [Argir](/wiki/Argir "Argir").{#ooui-php-1}

The [Norse](/wiki/Norsemen "Norsemen") ([Scandinavians](/wiki/Scandinavians "Scandinavians")) established their parliament on the [Tinganes](/wiki/Tinganes "Tinganes") peninsula in AD 850.^[\[2\]](#cite_note-2)^ Tórshavn thus became the capital of the Faroe Islands and has remained so ever since. Early on, Tórshavn became the centre of the islands' trade monopoly, thereby being the only legal place for the islanders to sell and buy goods. In 1856, the trade monopoly was abolished and the islands were left open to free trade.{#cite_ref-2}

### Early history {#Early_history}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=T%C3%B3rshavn&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Early history")\]

It is not known whether the site of Tórshavn was of interest to the Irish monks who were [probably the first settlers](/wiki/History_of_the_Faroe_Islands "History of the Faroe Islands") in the Faroes. The Viking settlers in the 9th century established their own parliaments, called *[tings](/wiki/Thing_(assembly) "Thing (assembly)")* , in different parts of the islands, it being the tradition in each case to hold the *ting* at a neutral and thus uninhabited place, so no one location gave anyone an advantage. According to romantics, the main *ting* for the islands was convoked in Tórshavn in 825, on [Tinganes](/wiki/Tinganes "Tinganes"),^[\[3\]](#cite_note-3)^ the [peninsula](/wiki/Peninsula "Peninsula") that divides the harbour into two parts: *Eystaravág* and *Vestaravág*.{#cite_ref-3}

The settlers would thus meet on the flat rocks of Tinganes every summer, as the most central place on the islands, although there was no settlement at Tinganes at that time. The [Færeyinga Saga](/wiki/F%C3%A6reyinga_Saga "Færeyinga Saga") says: "the place of the *ting* of the [Faroese](/wiki/Faroe_Islanders "Faroe Islanders") was on Streymoy, and there is the harbour that is called Tórshavn". The Viking age ended in 1035. The *ting* was followed by a market which gradually grew into a permanent trading area.

All through the [Middle Ages](/wiki/Middle_Ages "Middle Ages"), the narrow peninsula jutting out into the sea made up the main part of Tórshavn. It belonged to the outfield of two farmers. Unlike other Faroese villages, Tórshavn was never a distinct farming community. During the 12th century, all trade between Norway and the Faroes, along with other tributary islands to the west, became centralised in [Bergen](/wiki/Bergen "Bergen").

In 1271, a royal trade monopoly was established in Tórshavn by the Norwegian Crown. According to a document from 1271, two ships would sail regularly to Tórshavn from Bergen with cargoes of salt, timber and cereal. Tórshavn therefore had more contact with the outside world than the other villages did. Under the Norwegian, and then Danish rule, government officials made Tórshavn their home. All of these things, combined with the fact that Tórshavn was the seat of the *ting* of the islands, influenced the town's development.  

### 1500--1800 {#1500.E2.80.931800}

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/T%C3%B3rshavn_skansin_8.jpg/220px-T%C3%B3rshavn_skansin_8.jpg)](/wiki/File:T%C3%B3rshavn_skansin_8.jpg) [Skansin](/wiki/Skansin "Skansin") fort has been rebuilt several times since it was first built in 1580. The current building dates back to 1790

Sources do not mention a built-up area in Tórshavn until after the Protestant reformation in 1539. In  1580 a small fort, [Skansin](/wiki/Skansin "Skansin"), was built by the Faroese naval hero and trader [Magnus Heinason](/wiki/Magnus_Heinason "Magnus Heinason") at the north end of the harbour. Later, small fortifications were built at Tinganes.

In 1584, Tórshavn had 101 inhabitants. The population was divided into three equally large groups made up of farmers, their families and servants, trade and government officials and people who owned no land and therefore not much else; this included the landless [proletariat](/wiki/Proletariat "Proletariat") from the villages that during this period came to Tórshavn in search of work. They were set to guard duty on Skansin without pay, and for clothing and food they depended on the bounty of the farmers.

In 1655, king [Frederick III of Denmark](/wiki/Frederick_III_of_Denmark "Frederick III of Denmark") granted the Faroe Islands to his favourite statesman [Kristoffer Gabel](/wiki/Kristoffer_Gabel "Kristoffer Gabel"); the rule of the von Gabel Family (lasting between 1655 and 1709), is known as *Gablatíðin* . It is the darkest chapter in the history of Tórshavn. Gabel's administration suppressed the islanders in various ways. The trade monopoly was in the family's hands and it was not designed for the needs of the Faroese people. People across the country brought products into town and had to be satisfied with whatever price they were given. At the same time, imported goods were limited and expensive. There came considerable complaints from the islands' inhabitants of unjust treatment by the civil administration in Tórshavn. These not only included the persons in charge of the monopoly trade, but also the [bailiff](/wiki/Bailiff "Bailiff") and others. It was during this period, in 1673, that Tinganes was ravaged by a fire after a store of gunpowder kept at Tinganes had blown up. Many old houses burnt to the ground and old Faroese records were lost as were Gabel's documents.

Conditions improved in Tórshavn when the trade monopoly became a royal monopoly in 1709. The [Danish royal trade monopoly](/wiki/Danish_Royal_Trade_Monopoly_in_the_Faroe_Islands "Danish Royal Trade Monopoly in the Faroe Islands") was supplied with goods from [Copenhagen](/wiki/Copenhagen "Copenhagen") three times a year. However, Tórshavn was hit by a plague of smallpox in 1709, killing nearly the entire population. The town had dissipated reached a population of 300 before the outbreak; 250 of the inhabitants died from the disease. Still, it was during the latter half of the 18th century that Tórshavn started to develop into a small town. This was while Niels Ryberg was in charge of the trade monopoly. From 1768 (and during the next 20 years onwards) Ryberg was allowed to carry on an entrepot trade which was mainly based on smuggling to England. Because of the French-British conflict there was room for this kind of operation. In Tórshavn his warehouses filled up with goods. Ryberg was the first person who thought of making a financial profit from fishing, which later became the most important economic factor to the islands. He experimented with salted cod and herring but at this point in time nothing much beyond this happened.

[Tórshavn Cathedral](/wiki/T%C3%B3rshavn_Cathedral "Tórshavn Cathedral") was first built in 1788 and partly rebuilt in 1865. Since 1990, it has been the seat of the Bishop of the Faroe Islands (in the [Church of the Faroe Islands](/wiki/Church_of_the_Faroe_Islands "Church of the Faroe Islands")).  

### 1800--present {#1800.E2.80.93present}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=T%C3%B3rshavn&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: 1800–present")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Recherche_Expedition_1839_T%C3%B3rshavn.jpg/220px-Recherche_Expedition_1839_T%C3%B3rshavn.jpg)](/wiki/File:Recherche_Expedition_1839_T%C3%B3rshavn.jpg) Tórshavn in 1839, by Barthélemy Lauvergne

On 30 March 1808, during the Anglo-Danish [Gunboat War](/wiki/Gunboat_War "Gunboat War"), the [*Cruizer*-class brig-sloop](/wiki/Cruizer-class_brig-sloop "Cruizer-class brig-sloop") [HMS *Clio*](/wiki/HMS_Clio_(1807) "HMS Clio (1807)") entered Tórshavn and briefly captured the fort at Skansin. The fort surrendered without firing a shot as the landing party approached. The *Clio* 's men [spiked](/wiki/Touch_hole#Spiking_the_guns "Touch hole") the fort's eight 18-pounder guns and took all the smaller guns and weapons before leaving. Shortly after 6 May, a German privateer who had assumed the name "Baron von Hompesch" plundered the defenceless city and seized the property of the Danish Crown Monopoly. The Admiralty Prize Court, however, refused to condemn it as a lawful prize.^\[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Logting_1864%2C_faroe_islands.jpg/220px-Logting_1864%2C_faroe_islands.jpg)](/wiki/File:Logting_1864,_faroe_islands.jpg) Tórshavn in 1864, the [Løgting](/wiki/L%C3%B8gting "Løgting") (parliament) is at top left

In 1856, free trade came to the Faroe Islands. By opening the islands to the world, it transformed the economy, with Tórshavn at its centre. ^[\[4\]](#cite_note-4)^{#cite_ref-4}

In 1866, Tórshavn's town council was founded. The town has been the capital of the Faroe Islands ever since. Later, in 1909, Tórshavn became a market town with the same municipal charter as Danish market towns. In 1913, the Danish [Folketing](/wiki/Folketing "Folketing") granted DKK 810,000 to construction of a harbour in Tórshavn. Local waves are 3 m (9.8 ft)^\[*[clarification needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify "Wikipedia:Please clarify")*\]^, the waters are ice free and have a tidal variation of 0.3 m (1 ft 0 in), and storms from the west are mitigated by the gentle eastwards slope of the mountains. Other harbours were also benefitted with an 80% grant to a total build cost of DKK 1.6 million.^[\[5\]](#cite_note-ing1913-5)^ In 1927, Tórshavn had a modern harbour built. This made it possible for larger ships to berth.^[\[6\]](#cite_note-Gregoriussen_11-15-6)^{#cite_ref-Gregoriussen_11-15_6-0}

During the [British occupation of the Faroe Islands in World War II](/wiki/British_occupation_of_the_Faroe_Islands_in_World_War_II "British occupation of the Faroe Islands in World War II"), Skansin was used as the headquarters of the Royal Navy Command, and two 5.5-inch guns used aboard [HMS *Furious*](/wiki/HMS_Furious_(47) "HMS Furious (47)") before World War II were deployed.^[\[7\]](#cite_note-7)^{#cite_ref-7}

In 1974, the neighbouring villages [Hoyvík](/wiki/Hoyv%C3%ADk "Hoyvík") and [Hvítanes](/wiki/Hv%C3%ADtanes "Hvítanes") were made part of the town area. Later, even more municipalities joined the Tórshavn municipality. In 1978 Kaldbak, in 1997 Argir, in 2001 Kollafjørður, and finally in 2005, Kirkjubøur, Hestur, and Nólsoy.

Tórshavn features a [subpolar oceanic climate](/wiki/Subpolar_oceanic_climate "Subpolar oceanic climate") (*[Cfc](/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification "Köppen climate classification")* ), with strong moderation from the [Atlantic Ocean](/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean "Atlantic Ocean")'s [Norwegian Current](/wiki/Norwegian_Current "Norwegian Current"). In winter, Tórshavn tends to be under direct influence of the [Icelandic Low](/wiki/Icelandic_Low "Icelandic Low"), which usually brings overcast and stormy weather to the Faroe Islands. Because of its cloudiness and the ice-free water surrounding Tórshavn, its winter temperatures are exceptionally mild for such a northerly location, with winter daytime temperatures usually oscillating around 6 °C (43 °F). However, summer temperatures are much lower than those found in Scandinavia on similar latitudes, and barely exceed 13 °C (55 °F) daily highs in the warmest month. The moderation also causes the extremes amplitude to be very low: in the period from 1961 to 2021, there was a mere 33 °C (59 °F) difference between the absolute warmest and coldest temperatures. Temperatures below freezing may occur in any non-summer month, but even in winter, the average daily lows stay well above 0 °C (32 °F).

Average monthly precipitation is highest in autumn and winter, peaking in January, due to the activity of the [Icelandic Low](/wiki/Icelandic_Low "Icelandic Low"). May, June and July, on the other hand, are markedly drier but still receive substantial rainfall.

Politics and government {#Politics_and_government}
--------------------------------------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=T%C3%B3rshavn&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Politics and government")\]

Tórshavn is the capital of the Faroe Islands, and as such is the seat of the Faroes' [self rule government](/wiki/Cabinet_of_the_Faroe_Islands "Cabinet of the Faroe Islands"). The government holds the executive power in local government affairs. Today a part of the government is located on the [Tinganes](/wiki/Tinganes "Tinganes") peninsula of Tórshavn. The Prime Minister's office is there and the Ministry of Internal Affairs was also there until it was closed in 2013. The other ministries are located in other office buildings in various places in Tórshavn, i.e. the Ministry of Health^[\[12\]](#cite_note-12)^ and the Ministry of Social Affairs^[\[13\]](#cite_note-13)^ are located near the [Hospital of the Faroes](/w/index.php?title=Landssj%C3%BAkrah%C3%BAsi%C3%B0&action=edit&redlink=1 "Landssjúkrahúsið (page does not exist)") in Eirargarður, and the Ministry of Finance is located in [Argir](/wiki/Argir "Argir") in a building called Albert Hall on the street Kvíggjartún.^[\[14\]](#cite_note-fmr-14)^ The parliament, the [Løgting](/wiki/L%C3%B8gting "Løgting"), which was originally located on Tinganes, was relocated to the town square, Vaglið, in 1856.{#cite_ref-fmr_14-0}

Sport {#Sport}
--------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=T%C3%B3rshavn&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Sport")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Torsvollur_Football_Venue_in_Torshavn_Faroe_Islands.JPG/220px-Torsvollur_Football_Venue_in_Torshavn_Faroe_Islands.JPG)](/wiki/File:Torsvollur_Football_Venue_in_Torshavn_Faroe_Islands.JPG) [Tórsvøllur](/wiki/T%C3%B3rsv%C3%B8llur "Tórsvøllur"), the home stadium of the [Faroe Islands national football team](/wiki/Faroe_Islands_national_football_team "Faroe Islands national football team") [![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Drekin_a_Faroese_Row_Boat.jpg/220px-Drekin_a_Faroese_Row_Boat.jpg)](/wiki/File:Drekin_a_Faroese_Row_Boat.jpg) A boat race at the Jóansøka Festival in Tvøroyri in June 2011.

Tórshavn, as the capital city, is the centre of sport in the islands; the largest sports centre is located in the [Gundadalur](/wiki/Gundadalur "Gundadalur") district of Tórshavn. Also, the largest football stadium, [Tórsvøllur](/wiki/T%C3%B3rsv%C3%B8llur "Tórsvøllur"), is located here, seating 6,000 spectators. The stadium serves as home to the [Faroe Islands national football team](/wiki/Faroe_Islands_national_football_team "Faroe Islands national football team"). Around the city there are also two other football pitches, indoor tennis courts, badminton courts and a swimming pool.

The city has several football clubs, including three [Premier League](/wiki/Faroe_Islands_Premier_League "Faroe Islands Premier League") teams: [HB Tórshavn](/wiki/HB_T%C3%B3rshavn "HB Tórshavn"), [B36 Tórshavn](/wiki/B36_T%C3%B3rshavn "B36 Tórshavn") and [Argja Bóltfelag](/wiki/Argja_B%C3%B3ltfelag "Argja Bóltfelag"). Other football clubs with connections to the city are [FF Giza](/wiki/FF_Giza "FF Giza") ([Nólsoy](/wiki/N%C3%B3lsoy "Nólsoy")), [FC Hoyvík](/wiki/FC_Hoyv%C3%ADk "FC Hoyvík") and [Undrið FF](/wiki/Undri%C3%B0_FF "Undrið FF"). [Handball](/wiki/Team_handball "Team handball") is the second most popular sport in Tórshavn. The city's handball teams are [Kyndil](/wiki/Kyndil "Kyndil"), [Neistin](/wiki/Neistin "Neistin") and [Ítróttafelagið H71](/w/index.php?title=%C3%8Dtr%C3%B3ttafelagi%C3%B0_H71&action=edit&redlink=1 "Ítróttafelagið H71 (page does not exist)") and the [Faroe Island's national handball team](/wiki/Faroe_Islands_men%27s_national_handball_team "Faroe Islands men's national handball team") practice in the city. Tórshavn city has several popular [rowing](/wiki/Rowing_(sport) "Rowing (sport)") clubs, including, [Havnar Róðrarfelag](/wiki/Havnar_R%C3%B3%C3%B0rarfelag "Havnar Róðrarfelag") and [Róðrarfelagið Knørrur](/wiki/R%C3%B3%C3%B0rarfelagi%C3%B0_Kn%C3%B8rrur "Róðrarfelagið Knørrur").^[\[15\]](#cite_note-15)^{#cite_ref-15}

Every year in July the [Tour of Faroe Islands](/wiki/Tour_of_Faroe_Islands "Tour of Faroe Islands"), which is a [road bicycle race](/wiki/Road_bicycle_race "Road bicycle race"), is held around the islands. The race, called *Kring Føroyar* (Tour de Faroe / Around the Faroes), starts in [Klaksvík](/wiki/Klaksv%C3%ADk "Klaksvík") and ends in Tórshavn.^[\[16\]](#cite_note-16)^{#cite_ref-16}  

Music {#Music}
--------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=T%C3%B3rshavn&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Music")\]

The Tórshavn Jazz Festival has been held annually since 1983. It attracts musicians from all over North America and Europe and has become a popular tourist event.  

Transport {#Transport}
----------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=T%C3%B3rshavn&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: Transport")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Torshavn.png/170px-Torshavn.png)](/wiki/File:Torshavn.png) A map of Tórshavn showing road links  
See also: [Transport in the Faroe Islands](/wiki/Transport_in_the_Faroe_Islands "Transport in the Faroe Islands")

The harbour is served by the [Smyril Line](/wiki/Smyril_Line "Smyril Line") international ferry service to [Denmark](/wiki/Denmark "Denmark") and [Iceland](/wiki/Iceland "Iceland"). The harbour is also used by domestic ferry services of [Strandfaraskip Landsins](/wiki/Strandfaraskip_Landsins "Strandfaraskip Landsins") within the Faroe Islands, chiefly on the route to [Tvøroyri](/wiki/Tv%C3%B8royri "Tvøroyri").

The town is served by [Bussleiðin](/wiki/Transport_in_the_Faroe_Islands#Bus_services "Transport in the Faroe Islands") -- a network of local buses, with the service identified by its red livery. Bussleiðin has five routes and is operated under contract by Gundurs Bussar P/F. Buses within Tórshavn have been completely free of charge since 2007. In addition, there is a helipad by the coast.
