**Stanley** (also known as **Port Stanley** ) is the [capital city](/wiki/Capital_city "Capital city") of the [Falkland Islands](/wiki/Falkland_Islands "Falkland Islands"). It is located on the island of [East Falkland](/wiki/East_Falkland "East Falkland"), on a north-facing slope in one of the wettest parts of the islands. At the 2016 census, the city had a population of 2,460.^[\[1\]](#cite_note-name-1)^ The entire population of the Falkland Islands was 3,398 on Census Day -- 9 October 2016.{#cite_ref-name_1-0}

Stanley is represented by five of the eight elected members of the [Legislative Assembly of the Falkland Islands](/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_the_Falkland_Islands "Legislative Assembly of the Falkland Islands"): [Stacy Bragger](/wiki/Stacy_Bragger "Stacy Bragger"), [Barry Elsby](/wiki/Barry_Elsby "Barry Elsby"), [Mark Pollard](/wiki/Mark_Pollard "Mark Pollard"), [Roger Spink](/wiki/Roger_Spink "Roger Spink"), and [Leona Vidal Roberts](/wiki/Leona_Vidal_Roberts "Leona Vidal Roberts"). An elected Town Council of Stanley existed from 1948 to 1973.

On 14 June 2022, Stanley received [letters patent](/wiki/Letters_patent_(United_Kingdom) "Letters patent (United Kingdom)"), formally awarding it city status.

Stanley is the main shopping centre on the islands and the hub of East Falkland's road network. Attractions include the [Falkland Islands Museum](/wiki/Falkland_Islands_Museum "Falkland Islands Museum"), [Government House](/wiki/Government_House_(Falkland_Islands) "Government House (Falkland Islands)")---built in 1845 and home to the [Governor of the Falkland Islands](/wiki/Governor_of_the_Falkland_Islands "Governor of the Falkland Islands")---and a [golf course](/wiki/Golf_course "Golf course"), as well as a whale-bone arch, a [totem pole](/wiki/Totem_pole "Totem pole"), several [war memorials](/wiki/War_memorial "War memorial") and the [shipwrecks](/wiki/Shipwreck "Shipwreck") in its harbour. The [Falkland Islands Company](/wiki/Falkland_Islands_Company "Falkland Islands Company") owns several shops. Stanley has four pubs, 11 hotels and guesthouses, three restaurants, a [fish and chip](/wiki/Fish_and_chip "Fish and chip") shop and the main tourist office. There are three churches, including the [Anglican](/wiki/Anglican "Anglican") [Christ Church Cathedral](/wiki/Christ_Church_Cathedral_(Falkland_Islands) "Christ Church Cathedral (Falkland Islands)"), the southernmost Anglican cathedral in the world, and the Roman Catholic [St. Mary's Church](/wiki/St._Mary%27s_Church,_Falkland_Islands "St. Mary's Church, Falkland Islands"). A [bomb disposal](/wiki/Bomb_disposal "Bomb disposal") unit in the town is a legacy of the [Falklands War](/wiki/Falklands_War "Falklands War").

The town hall serves as a post office, [philatelic bureau](/wiki/Philatelic_bureau "Philatelic bureau"), law court and dance hall. The [police station](/wiki/Royal_Falkland_Islands_Police "Royal Falkland Islands Police") also contains the islands' only prison, with capacity for 13 inmates.
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/WhaleboneArchCathedral.JPG/220px-WhaleboneArchCathedral.JPG)](/wiki/File:WhaleboneArchCathedral.JPG) The [cathedral](/wiki/Christ_Church_Cathedral_(Falkland_Islands) "Christ Church Cathedral (Falkland Islands)") and whalebone arch

The community centre includes a swimming pool (the only public one in the islands), a sports centre, and school. A grass football pitch is located by the community centre. A separate building houses the college of further education and the library. A new sports centre is under construction to the south of the town centre, next to a newly-laid floodlit all-weather pitch.

Stanley Racecourse, located on the west side of Stanley, holds a two-day horse racing meeting every year on 26 and 27 December. The Christmas races have been held here for over 100 years.

Stanley Golf Course has an 18-hole course and a club house. It is also located to the west of Stanley.

[King Edward VII Memorial Hospital](/wiki/King_Edward_VII_Memorial_Hospital "King Edward VII Memorial Hospital") is the islands' main hospital, with doctors' practice and surgery, radiology department, dental surgery and emergency facilities.

The [Port Stanley Airport](/wiki/Port_Stanley_Airport "Port Stanley Airport") operates internal flights, and scheduled international passenger flights operate from the [RAF Mount Pleasant](/wiki/RAF_Mount_Pleasant "RAF Mount Pleasant") military airbase.

Stanley is also home to the [Falkland Islands Radio Station](/wiki/Falkland_Islands_Radio_Station "Falkland Islands Radio Station") (FIRS), the Stanley office of the [British Antarctic Survey](/wiki/British_Antarctic_Survey "British Antarctic Survey"), and the office of the weekly *[Penguin News](/wiki/Penguin_News "Penguin News")* newspaper.^[\[2\]](#cite_note-2)^{#cite_ref-2}  

History {#History}
------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Stanley,_Falkland_Islands&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: History")\]

The original capital of the islands was at [Port Louis](/wiki/Port_Louis,_Falkland_Islands "Port Louis, Falkland Islands") to the north of the present site of Stanley, on [Berkeley Sound](/wiki/Berkeley_Sound "Berkeley Sound"). Captains [Francis Crozier](/wiki/Francis_Crozier "Francis Crozier") and [James Clark Ross](/wiki/James_Clark_Ross "James Clark Ross") were recruited by Governor [Richard Moody](/wiki/Richard_Moody "Richard Moody") in his quest to find a new capital for The Falklands. Both Crozier and Ross (who are remembered in Crozier Place and Ross Road in Stanley) were among the [Royal Navy](/wiki/Royal_Navy "Royal Navy")'s most distinguished seafarers. They spent five months in the islands with their ships *[Terror](/wiki/HMS_Terror_(1813) "HMS Terror (1813)")* and *[Erebus](/wiki/HMS_Erebus_(1826) "HMS Erebus (1826)")* , later lost looking for the [Northwest Passage](/wiki/Northwest_Passage "Northwest Passage"). Governor Moody (after whom [Moody Brook](/wiki/Moody_Brook "Moody Brook") is named), however, decided to move the capital to [Port Jackson](/wiki/Stanley_Harbour "Stanley Harbour"), which was renamed "Stanley Harbour", after a survey. Stanley Harbour was considered to have a deeper anchorage for visiting ships. Not all the inhabitants were happy with the change; a JW Whitington is recorded as saying, "Of all the miserable bog holes, I believe that Mr Moody has selected one of the worst for the site of his town."^[\[3\]](#cite_note-Wagstaff2018-3)^{#cite_ref-Wagstaff2018_3-0}
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Edward_Gennys_Fanshawe%2C_Settlement_at_Port_Stanley%2C_Falkland_Islands%2C_May_1849.jpg/290px-Edward_Gennys_Fanshawe%2C_Settlement_at_Port_Stanley%2C_Falkland_Islands%2C_May_1849.jpg)](/wiki/File:Edward_Gennys_Fanshawe,_Settlement_at_Port_Stanley,_Falkland_Islands,_May_1849.jpg) Settlement at Port Stanley, May 1849, by [Edward Fanshawe](/wiki/Edward_Fanshawe "Edward Fanshawe")

Work on the settlement began in 1843 and it became the capital in July 1845. It was named after [Lord Stanley](/wiki/Edward_Smith-Stanley,_14th_Earl_of_Derby "Edward Smith-Stanley, 14th Earl of Derby"), [Secretary of State for War and the Colonies](/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_War_and_the_Colonies "Secretary of State for War and the Colonies") at the time. In 1849, 30 married [Chelsea Pensioners](/wiki/Chelsea_Pensioner "Chelsea Pensioner") were settled there to help with the defence of the islands and to develop the new settlement.

The settlement soon grew as a [deep-water port](/wiki/Deep-water_port "Deep-water port"), specialising at first in ship repairs; before the construction of the [Panama Canal](/wiki/Panama_Canal "Panama Canal"), Port Stanley was a major repair stop for ships travelling through the [Straits of Magellan](/wiki/Straits_of_Magellan "Straits of Magellan"). The rough waters and intense storms found at the tip of the continent forced many ships to Stanley Harbour, and the ship repair industry helped to drive the island economy. Later it became a base for [whaling](/wiki/Whaling "Whaling") and [sealing](/wiki/Seal_hunting "Seal hunting") in the South Atlantic and [Antarctic](/wiki/Antarctic "Antarctic").

Later still it was an important [coaling](/wiki/Coaling_(ships) "Coaling (ships)") station for the [Royal Navy](/wiki/Royal_Navy "Royal Navy"). This led to ships based here being involved in the [Battle of the Falkland Islands](/wiki/Battle_of_the_Falkland_Islands "Battle of the Falkland Islands") in the First World War, and the [Battle of the River Plate](/wiki/Battle_of_the_River_Plate "Battle of the River Plate") in the [Second World War](/wiki/Second_World_War "Second World War").

[Landslides](/wiki/Landslide "Landslide") caused by excessive [peat](/wiki/Peat "Peat") cutting destroyed part of the town in 1879 and again in 1886, which killed two people. At about midnight on 29 November 1878 a black moving mass, several feet high, was moving forwards at a rate of 4 miles per hour (6.4 km/h) or 5 miles per hour (8.0 km/h). The next morning the town was cut in two; the only way to travel between the two parts was by boat.^[\[4\]](#cite_note-4)^{#cite_ref-4}

During the [Second World War](/wiki/World_War_II "World War II"), a hulk in Stanley Harbour was used for interning the [British Fascist](/wiki/British_fascism "British fascism") and [Mosleyite](/wiki/Mosleyite "Mosleyite") [Jeffrey Hamm](/wiki/Jeffrey_Hamm "Jeffrey Hamm").^[\[5\]](#cite_note-5)^ A minor figure in the [British Union of Fascists](/wiki/British_Union_of_Fascists "British Union of Fascists") (BUF) at the time, Hamm moved to the [Falkland Islands](/wiki/Falkland_Islands "Falkland Islands") in 1939 to work as a teacher. He was arrested there (under [Defence Regulation 18B](/wiki/Defence_Regulation_18B "Defence Regulation 18B")) in 1940 for encouraging fascist views among his pupils and his BUF membership and later transferred to a prison camp in [South Africa](/wiki/South_Africa "South Africa").{#cite_ref-5}
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/FalklandsMemorial.JPG/220px-FalklandsMemorial.JPG)](/wiki/File:FalklandsMemorial.JPG) The [1982 Liberation Memorial](/wiki/1982_Liberation_Memorial "1982 Liberation Memorial"), Stanley

[Stanley Airport](/wiki/Port_Stanley_Airport "Port Stanley Airport") is used by internal flights and provides connections to British bases in [Antarctica](/wiki/Antarctica "Antarctica"). Flights to Argentina ended after the 1982 conflict. A weekly flight to [Punta Arenas](/wiki/Punta_Arenas,_Chile "Punta Arenas, Chile") in Chile commenced in 1993, which now operates out of [RAF Mount Pleasant](/wiki/RAF_Mount_Pleasant "RAF Mount Pleasant"). Scheduled passenger flights between the Mount Pleasant airfield and the UK are also operated twice a week by a civilian airline contractor on behalf of the [Royal Air Force](/wiki/Royal_Air_Force "Royal Air Force").

Stanley was occupied by [Argentine](/wiki/Argentina "Argentina") troops for about 10 weeks during the [Falklands War](/wiki/Falklands_War "Falklands War") in 1982. The Argentinians renamed the town Puerto Argentino, and although Spanish names for places in the Falklands were historically accepted as alternatives, this one is considered to be extremely offensive by many islanders.^[\[6\]](#cite_note-6)^ Stanley suffered considerable damage during the war, from both the Argentine occupation and the British naval shelling of the town, which killed three civilians. After the British secured the high ground around the town the Argentines surrendered with no fighting in the town itself. The beaches and land around it were heavily mined and some areas remain marked [minefields](/wiki/Land_mine "Land mine").{#cite_ref-6}

Since the Falklands War, Stanley has benefited from the growth of the fishing and tourism industries in the Islands. Stanley itself has developed greatly in that time, with the building of a large amount of residential housing, particularly to the east of the town centre. Stanley is now more than a third bigger than it was in 1982.

In 2022, as part of [Queen Elizabeth II's](/wiki/Elizabeth_II "Elizabeth II") [Platinum Jubilee Civic Honours](/wiki/Platinum_Jubilee_Civic_Honours "Platinum Jubilee Civic Honours"), Stanley was one of the successful bids for [city status](/wiki/City_status_in_the_United_Kingdom "City status in the United Kingdom"), coinciding with the 40th anniversary of the [invasion](/wiki/Argentine_invasion_of_the_Falkland_Islands "Argentine invasion of the Falkland Islands") and [liberation](/wiki/Argentine_surrender_in_the_Falklands_War "Argentine surrender in the Falklands War") of the port. On 14 June 2022, Stanley received [letters patent](/wiki/Letters_patent_(United_Kingdom) "Letters patent (United Kingdom)") from the monarch awarding city status.^[\[7\]](#cite_note-7)^^[\[8\]](#cite_note-8)^ The [Governor of the Falkland Islands](/wiki/Governor_of_the_Falkland_Islands "Governor of the Falkland Islands"), [Nigel Phillips](/wiki/Nigel_Phillips "Nigel Phillips"), read out the document outside the town hall on the same day.^[\[9\]](#cite_note-9)^{#cite_ref-9}  

Etymology {#Etymology}
----------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Stanley,_Falkland_Islands&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Etymology")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/14th_Earl_of_Derby.jpg/220px-14th_Earl_of_Derby.jpg)](/wiki/File:14th_Earl_of_Derby.jpg) Stanley is named after [Edward Smith-Stanley, 14th Earl of Derby](/wiki/Edward_Smith-Stanley,_14th_Earl_of_Derby "Edward Smith-Stanley, 14th Earl of Derby"), who never visited the islands.

A number of variants of the city's name have appeared in both English and Spanish. [Stanley Harbour](/wiki/Stanley_Harbour "Stanley Harbour") was originally known as "Port Jackson", and this name would have applied to the area before the town was built. Although the town is officially known as "Stanley", it is frequently referred to as "Port Stanley", especially in British reports about the Falklands War. This is in line with various other settlements around the islands, e.g. [Port Howard](/wiki/Port_Howard "Port Howard") and [Port Stephens](/wiki/Port_Stephens,_Falkland_Islands "Port Stephens, Falkland Islands"). However, "Stanley" without the "Port" prefix was established long before the war, and on 2 August 1956, the Officer Administering the Government of the Falkland Islands reported to the Secretary of State for the Colonies in London as follows:
There is some difficulty over the correct name of the capital. Early despatches contain reference to both Port Stanley and Stanley. Port Stanley was accepted by the Naming Commission set up in 1943 to consider the names then being included on the War Office maps. Local opinion differs on the matter, but there is no doubt that Stanley is now common usage and has been for some considerable time. The capital is defined as Stanley in the Interpretation and General Law Ordinance. In the circumstances I would advise that the correct name for the capital is Stanley.^[\[10\]](#cite_note-FalklandTopnymy-10)^{#cite_ref-FalklandTopnymy_10-0}

The situation with the Spanish version of the name is far more complicated. Stanley, unlike [Port Louis](/wiki/Port_Louis,_Falkland_Islands "Port Louis, Falkland Islands"), the former capital of the islands, was a new settlement founded by the British, and therefore did not have a Spanish name of its own. Many Spanish speakers use "Puerto Stanley", as a neutral translation of the British name but it is disliked by supporters of Argentine sovereignty who refuse to recognise English language names. Supporters of the Argentine claim have used several different names, none of which are accepted by the islanders themselves:

* Puerto Soledad (the port of [East Falkland](/wiki/East_Falkland "East Falkland"), known in Spanish as *Isla Soledad*) -- reported in 1965, but in fact the old Spanish name of Port Louis, the old capital, not Stanley.
* Puerto Rivero -- a reference to [Antonio Rivero](/wiki/Antonio_Rivero "Antonio Rivero"), a controversial Argentine figure in the early history of the islands. Used by [peronists](/wiki/Peronism "Peronism") and the hijackers of [Aerolineas Argentinas Flight 648](/wiki/Aerolineas_Argentinas_Flight_648 "Aerolineas Argentinas Flight 648") who landed at Stanley in September 1966. It was also used on 3--4 April 1982 after the Argentine invasion.
* Puerto de la Isla Soledad -- a variant on Puerto Soledad. Used 5 April 1982.
* Puerto de las Islas Malvinas (Port of the Malvinas/Falklands), used from 6--20 April 1982.
* Puerto Argentino (Port Argentine) -- used ever since 21 April 1982 by the Argentines, although in 1994 the Argentine Government signed an undertaking to review toponymy relating to the Argentine occupation of the islands.

During the 1982 occupation, Patrick Watts of the islands' radio station used circumlocutions to avoid using Argentine names:
> "It hurt me greatly to call it \[the radio station\] Radio Nacional Islas Malvinas, and I used to try to avoid referring to Port Stanley as Puerto Argentino. I called it 'the capital' or the 'largest settlement on the island'"  
--- *Eyewitness Falklands: A personal account of the Falklands campaign*  

Climate {#Climate}
------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Stanley,_Falkland_Islands&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Climate")\]

The climate of Stanley is classified as a [subpolar oceanic](/wiki/Subpolar_oceanic "Subpolar oceanic") climate ([Köppen](/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification "Köppen climate classification") *Cfc* ), bordering very closely on a [polar climate](/wiki/Polar_climate "Polar climate") (*ET* ). Nowadays it barely avoids classification as *ET* because the mean temperature is greater than 10 °C (50 °F) for two months of the year.^[\[11\]](#cite_note-WeatherSpark-11)^ Unlike typical tundra climates, however, the winters are very mild, and vegetation grows there that normally could not in a climate this close to a polar climate. Contrast this with [Churchill](/wiki/Churchill,_Manitoba "Churchill, Manitoba"), Manitoba, which also has a near-tundra climate but is much more continental in nature.{#cite_ref-WeatherSpark_11-0}

The Falkland Islands have displayed a warming trend in recent years; the mean daily January maximum for Mount Pleasant for the years 1999-2012 is 15.1 °C (59.2 °F) compared to Stanley's 1961-90 average of 14.1 °C (57.4 °F).^[\[12\]](#cite_note-12)^ Formerly, Stanley had a [tundra climate](/wiki/Tundra_climate "Tundra climate") (*ET* ), due to cool summer temperatures (the mean temperature was less than 10 °C or 50 °F in the hottest month).^[\[13\]](#cite_note-Peel-13)^{#cite_ref-Peel_13-0}

Like the rest of the archipelago, Stanley has more or less even temperatures through the year and strong [westerlies](/wiki/Westerlies "Westerlies"). Precipitation, averaging 544 mm (21.42 in) a year, is nonetheless relatively low, and evenly spread throughout the year. Typically, at least 1 mm (0.039 in) of rain will be recorded on 125.2 days of the year. The islands receive 36.3% of possible sunshine, or around 1500--1600 hours a year, a level similar to southern parts of England. Daytime temperatures are similar to the Northern Isles of Scotland, though nights tend to be somewhat colder, with frost occurring on more than 1 in 3 nights (128.4 nights). Snow occurs in the winter. Light snowfall can occur at any time of year.

Stanley is a similar distance from the equator to British warm-summer climates like [London](/wiki/London "London"), [Cardiff](/wiki/Cardiff "Cardiff") and [Bristol](/wiki/Bristol "Bristol"), illustrating the relative chilliness of the climate. In the northern hemisphere, lowland tundra areas are located at latitudes further from the tropics. Many European capitals are also located much farther from the tropics than Stanley is. The nearest larger city of [Río Gallegos](/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Gallegos,_Santa_Cruz "Río Gallegos, Santa Cruz") (the provincial capital of [Santa Cruz](/wiki/Santa_Cruz_Province,_Argentina "Santa Cruz Province, Argentina")) in Argentina has a slightly milder climate (annual mean temperature being 1.7 °C (3.1 °F) higher) due to its position on the South American mainland, although summers everywhere on this latitude in the southern hemisphere are very cool due to important marine effects.

Temperature extremes at Stanley vary from −11.1 °C (12.0 °F) to 26.1 °C (79.0 °F).^[\[14\]](#cite_note-14)^^\[*[better source needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS "Wikipedia:NOTRS")*\]^^\[*[self-published source](/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Self-published_sources "Wikipedia:Verifiability")*\]^ More recently, on 23 January 1992, nearby Mount Pleasant Airport recorded 29.2 °C (84.6 °F).^[\[15\]](#cite_note-15)^{#cite_ref-15}

Education {#Education}
----------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Stanley,_Falkland_Islands&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Education")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Falkland_Islands_Community_School_in_2019_01.jpg/220px-Falkland_Islands_Community_School_in_2019_01.jpg)](/wiki/File:Falkland_Islands_Community_School_in_2019_01.jpg) [Falkland Islands Community School](/wiki/Falkland_Islands_Community_School "Falkland Islands Community School")

The Stanley Infant \& Junior School (IJS) is located along John Street at the intersection with Villiers Street in Stanley. The school first opened in 1955 and has about 250 students between the ages of four and 11.^[\[17\]](#cite_note-17)^{#cite_ref-17}

The [Falkland Island Community School](/wiki/Falkland_Island_Community_School "Falkland Island Community School") (FICS) is located on Reservoir Road in Stanley. It has approximately 220 students between 11 and 16.

Economy {#Economy}
------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Stanley,_Falkland_Islands&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Economy")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Welcome_to_Stanley_sign_with_background.jpg/310px-Welcome_to_Stanley_sign_with_background.jpg)](/wiki/File:Welcome_to_Stanley_sign_with_background.jpg) 'Welcome to Stanley' sign, with Stanley in the background

Today, roughly one third of the city's residents are employed by the government, and tourism is also a major source of employment. On days when two or more large cruise ships dock in the town, tourists frequently outnumber the local residents.

[Peat](/wiki/Peat "Peat") was once a prominent heating/fuel source in Stanley, and stacks of drying peat under cover can still be seen by the occasional house.

Gypsy Cove is four miles (6 km) from Stanley and can be reached by taxi or on foot.

* [Mensun Bound](/wiki/Mensun_Bound "Mensun Bound") (born 1953 in Port Stanley) is a British marine archaeologist, based in Oxford, Triton Senior Research Fellow in Marine Archaeology at Oxford University
* [James Peck](/wiki/James_Peck_(artist) "James Peck (artist)") (born 1968 in Stanley), an artist and writer who at one time held both Argentine and British citizenship but has since renounced his Argentine citizenship.^[\[20\]](#cite_note-20)^{#cite_ref-20}

### Modern-day politicians {#Modern-day_politicians}

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Stanley,_Falkland_Islands&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: Modern-day politicians")\]

* [Janet Lynda Cheek](/wiki/Jan_Cheek "Jan Cheek") (born 1948 in Stanley) is a politician, served as a [MLA](/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_the_Falkland_Islands "Legislative Assembly of the Falkland Islands") for the Stanley constituency from 1997 to 2005 and from 2009 to 2017
* [Mike Summers](/wiki/Mike_Summers "Mike Summers") OBE (born 1952 in Stanley) is a politician, served as a [MLA](/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_the_Falkland_Islands "Legislative Assembly of the Falkland Islands") for the Stanley constituency from 2011 to 2017
* [Sharon Halford](/wiki/Sharon_Halford "Sharon Halford") (born 1953 in Stanley) is a politician, served as a [MLA](/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_the_Falkland_Islands "Legislative Assembly of the Falkland Islands") for the Camp constituency from 1993 to 2001 and from 2009 until 2013
* [Glenn Ross](/wiki/Glenn_Ross_(politician) "Glenn Ross (politician)") (born 1964 in Stanley) is an engineer and politician, served as a [MLA](/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_the_Falkland_Islands "Legislative Assembly of the Falkland Islands") for the Stanley constituency from 2009 to 2011
* [Michael Poole](/wiki/Michael_Poole_(politician) "Michael Poole (politician)") (born 1984 in Falkland Islands) is a politician, served as a [MLA](/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_the_Falkland_Islands "Legislative Assembly of the Falkland Islands") for the Stanley constituency from 2013 to 2017
* [Gavin Short](/wiki/Gavin_Short "Gavin Short") (born 1962 in Falkland Islands) is a politician and served as a [MLA](/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_the_Falkland_Islands "Legislative Assembly of the Falkland Islands") for the Stanley constituency between 2009 and 2017. He is currently a senior news reporter with [FIBS](/wiki/Falkland_Islands_Radio_Service "Falkland Islands Radio Service") and was re-elected in the 2021 elections.
