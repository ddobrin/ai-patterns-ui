**St. Peter Port** ([French](/wiki/French_language "French language"): *Saint-Pierre Port* ) is a town and one of the [ten parishes](/wiki/Parishes_of_Guernsey "Parishes of Guernsey") on the island of [Guernsey](/wiki/Guernsey "Guernsey") in the [Channel Islands](/wiki/Channel_Islands "Channel Islands"). It is the [capital](/wiki/Capital_city "Capital city") of the [Bailiwick of Guernsey](/wiki/Bailiwick_of_Guernsey "Bailiwick of Guernsey") as well as the main [port](/wiki/Port "Port"). The population in 2019 was 18,958.

St. Peter Port is a small town (commonly referred to by locals as just "town")^[\[1\]](#cite_note-1)^ consisting mostly of steep, narrow streets and steps on the overlooking slopes. It is known that a trading post/town existed here before Roman times with a pre-Christian name which has not survived.{#cite_ref-1}

The parish covers an area of 6.5 km^2^. The [postal code](/wiki/GY_postcode_area "GY postcode area") for addresses in the parish starts with GY1.

People from St. Peter Port were nicknamed "les Villais" (the townspeople) or "cllichards" in [Guernésiais](/wiki/Guern%C3%A9siais "Guernésiais").  

Geography {#Geography}
----------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Saint_Peter_Port&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Geography")\]

St. Peter Port is on the east coast of [Guernsey](/wiki/Guernsey "Guernsey") overlooking [Herm](/wiki/Herm "Herm") and the tiny [Jethou](/wiki/Jethou "Jethou"); a further channel separates [Sark](/wiki/Sark "Sark") and surrounding islets such as [Brecqhou](/wiki/Brecqhou "Brecqhou"); [Normandy](/wiki/Normandy "Normandy")'s long [Cotentin Peninsula](/wiki/Cotentin_Peninsula "Cotentin Peninsula") and, to the south-east, [Jersey](/wiki/Jersey "Jersey") are visible in very clear conditions from some of the town's highest vantage points. The parish borders [St. Sampson](/wiki/St_Sampson%27s,_Guernsey "St Sampson's, Guernsey") in the north, The [Vale](/wiki/Vale,_Guernsey "Vale, Guernsey") in the north-west, [St. Andrew](/wiki/St_Andrew%27s,_Guernsey "St Andrew's, Guernsey") in the west and [St. Martin](/wiki/St_Martin%27s,_Guernsey "St Martin's, Guernsey") in the south.

The name of the nearest channel is the [Little Russel](/wiki/Little_Russel "Little Russel"), in which sits the [Bréhon Tower](/wiki/Br%C3%A9hon_Tower "Bréhon Tower"); that separating Sark is the [Big Russel](/wiki/Big_Russel "Big Russel"). Both in width are less than half of Guernsey's greatest length and dotted with coastal rocks and [stacks](/wiki/Stack_(geology) "Stack (geology)") quite near those islands, some of which are narrowly submerged.


Relief

The land in the north and by the harbour is low-lying but not marshy. In the south, the land gets higher (but not as high as [St Martin's](/wiki/St_Martin%27s,_Guernsey "St Martin's, Guernsey") or the [Forest](/wiki/Forest,_Guernsey "Forest, Guernsey")). Grassy, gently terraced cliffs behind sea walls (including projections) topped by trees characterise the southern part of the coast here (by an underground military museum and historic battery at semi-wooded **Havelet**). In the north is the more built-up Admiral Park development. To the south of the town lies Havelet Bay and the coastal path which leads to the very pretty Fermain Bay after a walk of about 20--25 minutes.

Subdivisions {#Subdivisions}
----------------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Saint_Peter_Port&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Subdivisions")\]
[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Castle_Cornet_Guernsey.jpg/280px-Castle_Cornet_Guernsey.jpg)](/wiki/File:Castle_Cornet_Guernsey.jpg) [Castle Cornet](/wiki/Castle_Cornet "Castle Cornet")

Saint Peter Port is subdivided into four cantons:^[\[4\]](#cite_note-4)^{#cite_ref-4}

1. Canton 1 or North Canton
2. Canton 2 or Canton of the North-West
3. Canton 3 or Canton of the South-West
4. Canton 4 or Canton of the South

In addition, the islands of [Herm](/wiki/Herm "Herm") and [Jethou](/wiki/Jethou "Jethou") belong to the parish,^[\[5\]](#cite_note-5)^ but are not part of any canton. They belong to Electoral district Saint Peter Port South.^[\[6\]](#cite_note-6)^{#cite_ref-6}

Main roads {#Main_roads}
------------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Saint_Peter_Port&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Main roads")\]

The following main roads (listed from north-south) provide important links between St Peter Port and the other parishes:

* Les Banques (leads up the coast to [St Sampson's](/wiki/St_Sampson%27s,_Guernsey "St Sampson's, Guernsey") and the north of the island)
* Rohais (leads to the parish of [Castel](/wiki/Castel,_Guernsey "Castel, Guernsey"))
* Mount Row (leads to [St Andrew's](/wiki/St_Andrew%27s,_Guernsey "St Andrew's, Guernsey") and the west of the island)
* Ruette Brayes (leads to [St Martin's](/wiki/St_Martin%27s,_Guernsey "St Martin's, Guernsey") and the south of the island)
* Fort Road (leads to [St Martin's](/wiki/St_Martin%27s,_Guernsey "St Martin's, Guernsey"))

The following coastal roads (listed from north to south) are also very important as they provide access to the shops, carparks and the [harbour](/wiki/St_Peter_Port_Harbour "St Peter Port Harbour"):

* St George's Esplanade
* North Esplanade
* South Esplanade

Politics {#Politics}
--------------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Saint_Peter_Port&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Politics")\]

Saint Peter Port comprises two [administrative division](/wiki/Politics_of_Guernsey#Administrative_divisions "Politics of Guernsey"), St Peter Port South and St Peter Port North.

In the [2016 Guernsey general election](/wiki/2016_Guernsey_general_election "2016 Guernsey general election") in:

* St Peter Port South there was a 2,068 or 63% turnout to elect five Deputies
* St Peter Port North there was a 2,639 or 65% turnout to elect six Deputies.
